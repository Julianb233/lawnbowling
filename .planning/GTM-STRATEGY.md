## Executive Summary

LawnBowl is a club management platform purpose-built for lawn bowling clubs, launching as an iOS app and PWA. The app replaces paper draw sheets, manual scoring, and fragmented club administration with an iPad kiosk-first experience designed for the 60-85 year old demographic that dominates the sport.

**The Opportunity:** There are approximately 6,000+ lawn bowling clubs globally across our target markets, with over 500,000 registered participants. No single platform serves the end-to-end needs of club directors running tournament-day operations. BowlsLink (the Bowls Australia official platform) has a 3.8-star App Store rating, frustrating UX, and is limited to the Australian market. Other solutions are fragmented, desktop-only, or require physical dongles.

**The Strategy:** Land and expand through Australian clubs first (2,000+ clubs, 240,000 participants), then replicate across English-speaking bowling nations. Start free, build density in club networks through word-of-mouth, then monetise through club subscriptions and association licensing.

**Unfair Advantage:** Built FOR bowlers BY bowlers. iPad kiosk-first. Uses real lawn bowling terminology. 122 pages of purpose-built functionality. No other app provides the full tournament-day workflow: kiosk check-in, position preferences, automatic draw generation, live scoring, and results -- all in one platform.

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)

**Conservative TAM calculation:**

- 6,950 clubs x average AUD $500/year subscription = **AUD $3.48M/year** (club subscriptions)
- 690,000 players x AUD $5/year per-player fee = **AUD $3.45M/year** (player fees)
- Equipment affiliate revenue (Big 4 manufacturers: Henselite, Aero, Taylor, Drakes Pride) = **AUD $500K/year**
- Insurance referral revenue (BCIB, Sportscover) = **AUD $300K/year**
- **Total TAM: ~AUD $7.7M/year**

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):**
- Australia-only (Phase 1): 2,000 clubs x $500 = **AUD $1M/year**
- Plus player fees: 240,000 x $5 = **AUD $1.2M/year**
- **SAM: ~AUD $2.2M/year**

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) -- Year 1:**
- Target: 200 clubs (10% of Australian market) = **AUD $100K-200K ARR**

### Market Dynamics

**Declining but digitising:** Traditional lawn bowls participation is in a slow decline globally. Bowls England has lost 100+ clubs in four years, and 85%+ of members are over 55. However, "barefoot bowls" and social bowls events are driving new interest, particularly among under-35s in Australia.

**Digital adoption among seniors is surging:** 76% of 65+ year olds now own smartphones (Pew, 2024). In Australia, 63% of seniors rate their mobile abilities as "good to excellent." This is the right moment to bring clubs online.

**Clubs are desperate for volunteers:** Club secretaries and directors are overwhelmed. Paper-based systems require manual effort that shrinking volunteer pools cannot sustain. Digital tools that save time are not optional -- they are survival tools.

### Key Competitive Gaps We Exploit

1. **No competitor offers the full tournament-day workflow** (kiosk check-in -> position preferences -> draw generation -> scoring -> results) in one app
2. **No competitor is iPad kiosk-first** -- club directors need a device on the counter, not a phone app
3. **BowlsLink has terrible UX for elderly users** -- slow 2FA, no FaceID, unintuitive navigation
4. **Desktop tools (PAMI, Drawmaker) are dying** -- dongle-based, Windows-only, no cloud sync
5. **UK tools (Bowlr, BowlsManager) don't serve Australia** and lack tournament operations
6. **Generic tools (PlayPass) don't speak bowls language** -- they don't know what a "skip" or "rink" is
7. **No competitor has a club directory with map** -- bowlers can't easily find clubs near them
8. **No competitor integrates insurance** -- our DEI integration is unique

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary: Club Directors / Secretaries (Decision Makers)

- **Who:** Volunteer club administrators, aged 55-75, managing 50-500 members
- **Pain points:** Drowning in paper, spending hours on draw sheets, manually tracking scores, can't get enough volunteers
- **What they need:** "Something that just works" -- minimal setup, big text, obvious buttons
- **How to reach:** State association channels, club secretary networks, word of mouth
- **Buying trigger:** Seeing another club use it successfully

### Secondary: Active Players (Users)

- **Who:** Regular competitive bowlers, aged 60-85, playing 2-4 times per week
- **Pain points:** Never know when results are posted, can't see draws until they arrive at the club, want to track their stats
- **What they need:** Simple read-only access to draws, scores, and club info
- **How to reach:** Through their club director adopting the platform

### Tertiary: New / Social Bowlers (Growth Segment)

- **Who:** 25-55 year olds discovering barefoot/social bowls
- **Pain points:** Don't know where to play, intimidated by formal clubs
- **What they need:** Club directory, event discovery, low-friction onboarding
- **How to reach:** Social media, Google search, app store discovery

### Quaternary: State/National Associations (Licensing Partners)

- **Who:** Bowls Australia, Bowls NSW, Bowls Victoria, Bowls England, etc.
- **Pain points:** BowlsLink frustrates clubs, fragmented data, can't track participation nationally
- **What they need:** A white-label or integrated platform they can endorse
- **How to reach:** Direct outreach, demonstrate club adoption, prove value

## Customer Acquisition Strategy

### Phase 1: Seed Clubs (Months 1-3)
**Goal: 10 clubs actively using the platform**

**Tactics:**
1. **Personal outreach to 20 clubs in Sydney/Melbourne metro areas**
   - Target clubs with active Facebook pages (indicates digital willingness)
   - Offer completely free access for 12 months as founding clubs
   - Provide on-site setup assistance (drive to the club, set up their iPad)
   - Ask for nothing except honest feedback and a testimonial if they like it

2. **RSL club network**
   - NSW alone has 1,621 bowling greens across RSL and community clubs
   - The 57th National RSL Lawn Bowls Carnival is in Melbourne 2026 -- attend and demonstrate
   - RSL clubs have bigger budgets and more structured operations

3. **"One Club Wonder" demos**
   - Run a complete tournament day at a club using LawnBowl
   - Film the whole process (consent from participants)
   - Create a 3-minute case study video showing before (paper) vs. after (iPad kiosk)

**Success Metrics:**
- 10 clubs onboarded
- 500+ individual player accounts created
- 3 video testimonials captured
- Net Promoter Score (NPS) > 40

### Phase 2: Word of Mouth (Months 3-6)
**Goal: 50 clubs, organic growth through bowler networks**

**Tactics:**
1. **Club-to-club referral program**
   - When a club director recommends LawnBowl to another club, both get an extra month free on their future paid plan
   - Provide referral cards (physical, business-card sized) for directors to hand out at inter-club competitions
   - Inter-club pennant competitions are the primary viral vector -- players from different clubs play together

2. **Facebook group seeding**
   - Post weekly in Lawn Bowlers Australia (19K members)
   - Share genuine stories from seed clubs (not ads -- stories)
   - Respond to every complaint about paper draw sheets or BowlsLink with a helpful comment (not a sales pitch)

3. **Club noticeboard poster program**
   - Design simple A4 posters: "Your club is now on LawnBowl" with QR code
   - Send 5 printed posters to every club that signs up
   - Include fridge magnets with the app URL for members

4. **State association engagement**
   - Present at Bowls NSW club director forums
   - Offer free trials to all clubs in one region (e.g., all of the Illawarra District)
   - Provide participation data reports that associations crave

**Success Metrics:**
- 50 clubs onboarded
- 30% organic/referral acquisition rate
- 2,500+ individual player accounts
- 1 state association pilot partnership

### Phase 3: Association Partnerships (Months 6-12)
**Goal: 200 clubs, official association endorsement**

**Tactics:**
1. **"Official App" pitch to state associations**
   - Present club adoption data and satisfaction scores
   - Offer white-label option with association branding
   - Provide aggregated participation data (which associations desperately need)
   - Undercut BowlsLink by offering better UX and responsive support

2. **Integration with BowlsLink results database**
   - Build an API integration to push results to BowlsLink (if they have one)
   - Position LawnBowl as the club-level interface that feeds into the national system
   - This removes the "but our results need to go to BowlsLink" objection

3. **District-level rollouts**
   - Target entire bowling districts (e.g., all clubs in Bowls Inner West, Sydney)
   - Offer district pricing: all clubs in a district get a group rate
   - District-level adoption creates network effects for pennant competitions

**Success Metrics:**
- 200 clubs onboarded
- 1+ official state association partnership
- 10,000+ individual player accounts
- AUD $100K ARR

### Phase 4: International Expansion (Months 12-24)
**Goal: Enter UK and NZ markets**

**Tactics:**
1. **UK launch via Bowls England connection**
   - 2,700 clubs in England alone, 3,500+ across UK
   - Adapt terminology where needed (minimal -- bowls language is universal)
   - Partner with Bowlr or position as their tournament-day complement

2. **NZ launch**
   - 610 clubs, strong Australian cultural connection
   - Many NZ bowlers compete in Australian events -- they will have already seen LawnBowl
   - Bowls New Zealand is a smaller, more accessible organization

3. **South Africa, Canada as secondary markets**
   - South Africa: 513 clubs, strong bowls culture
   - Canada: 271 clubs, seasonal (outdoor season only in most provinces)

## Pricing & Revenue Model

### Recommended Pricing Structure

**Freemium model with club-level subscriptions:**

| Tier | Price | Features | Target |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Free** | $0/forever | Club directory listing, basic member profiles (up to 30), 1 admin user, community features | Small clubs, social bowls groups |
| **Club** | AUD $29/month (AUD $290/year) | Unlimited members, 3 admin users, kiosk check-in, draw generation, scoring, results history, email notifications | Standard clubs with regular competitions |
| **Club Pro** | AUD $49/month (AUD $490/year) | Everything in Club, plus: unlimited admins, advanced analytics, pennant management, multi-green support, priority support, custom branding | Large clubs, multi-green facilities |
| **Association** | AUD $199/month (custom) | White-label option, district-wide dashboard, aggregated participation data, API access, dedicated support | State/district associations |

### Pricing Justification

- **BowlsManager (UK):** GBP 415/year + GBP 175 setup = ~AUD $800/year
- **Bowlr (UK):** From GBP 8/month = ~AUD $150/year minimum
- **BowlsLink:** Free (association-funded, but clubs pay through association fees)
- **PAMI:** Unknown (dongle + license)
- **PlayPass:** From $10.79/month = ~AUD $130/year

Our Club tier at AUD $290/year is:
- **Cheaper than BowlsManager** (AUD $800/yr)
- **Comparable to Bowlr** (AUD $150/yr) but with far more features
- **More valuable than BowlsLink** (free but frustrating -- clubs will pay for something that works)

### Additional Revenue Streams

1. **Equipment shop affiliate revenue** (5-10% commission from Henselite, Aero, Taylor, Drakes Pride sales)
2. **Insurance referral fees** (per-policy referral fee from BCIB or Sportscover)
3. **Tournament entry processing fees** (1.5-2% payment processing on tournament entries)
4. **Sponsored club directory listings** (equipment shops, coaches can pay for featured listings)
5. **Data insights** (anonymised, aggregated participation data sold to associations and equipment manufacturers)

## Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### High Risk

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Club directors resist technology adoption** | High | High | iPad kiosk removes the "app" barrier -- it's just a screen on the counter. On-site setup assistance. Phone support (not email). Large text, simple flows. "If you can use an iPad, you can use LawnBowl." |
| **BowlsLink improves and clubs stay** | Medium | High | BowlsLink is association-funded and moves slowly. Our speed of iteration is the moat. Ship weekly improvements based on real club feedback. Focus on tournament-day workflow where BowlsLink is weakest. |
| **Global Lawn Bowls (GLB) gains APAC traction** | Medium | Medium | GLB is focused on association-level deals. We focus on individual club adoption (bottom-up vs. top-down). Our club directory and content ecosystem create switching costs. |
| **Low conversion from free to paid** | Medium | High | Free tier must be genuinely useful (not crippled). Paid tier must have clear, visible value (more members, more admins, kiosk mode). Run conversion experiments early. |

### Medium Risk

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Privacy concerns from elderly users** | Medium | Medium | Clear, simple privacy policy. No data selling. All data belongs to the club. Prominent "we never share your information" messaging. GDPR-compliant for UK expansion. |
| **Accessibility failures** | Medium | Medium | Already targeting 44px minimum touch targets. Add screen reader support. Test with actual 70+ year old users. Follow WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. |
| **Seasonal usage (outdoor bowls is seasonal in some markets)** | Medium | Low | Australia has year-round bowls in most states. UK/Canada are seasonal -- plan for this in pricing (don't charge for unused months, or offer annual pricing). |
| **Apple App Store rejection** | Low | High | PWA is the fallback. App is a wrapper around the PWA via Capacitor -- lower rejection risk than a fully native app. Follow Apple's guidelines carefully. |
| **Competitors copy our features** | High | Low | Speed is the moat. Ship weekly. Build community (hard to copy). Accumulate club data and network effects (harder to copy). |

### What Features Are Bowlers Asking For (That Nobody Provides)?

Based on research across forums, app reviews, and club feedback:

1. **Real-time draw visibility** -- "I want to see my draw on my phone before I get to the club"
2. **Automatic balanced draws** -- "The draw sheet always has the same people together"
3. **Position preference tracking** -- "I always want to play lead but I keep getting put at skip"
4. **Quick guest check-in** -- "We have visitors every week and the signup process is terrible"
5. **Score history and personal stats** -- "I want to know my win rate this season"
6. **Club finder with map** -- "New bowlers can't find us -- we're hidden behind the RSL"
7. **Inter-club communication** -- "Organising practice matches between clubs is a nightmare"
8. **Weather integration** -- "Cancel notifications when it's raining instead of people driving to the club"
