## 1. Dropshipping Lawn Bowling Gear

### 1.1 The Equipment Landscape

#### Bowls (The Balls) -- The Big Four Manufacturers

| Brand | Origin | Popular Models | Set of 4 Price (USD) | Best For | Notes |
|-------|--------|---------------|---------------------|----------|-------|
| **Henselite** | Australia | Tiger, Tiger II, Tiger Evo, Tiger Pro, Dreamline XG | $600-$650+ | All levels; Tiger II is versatile for beginners | 10-year guarantee. Mega Grip technology. Sizes 000-5. Up to 13 color options. Custom orders take up to 4 months. |
| **Taylor** | Scotland (family-owned) | Ace, Blaze, Vector VS, International | $400-$600 | Leads (Ace/Blaze); all-rounders (Vector VS) | Oldest brand. Precision engineering. Distributed in USA via Bowls America. |
| **Drakes Pride** | UK | Professional, XP, Pro-50, Classic | $400-$550 (Pro-50 ~$500/GBP399) | Club players; wide range | Preferred supplier for many UK clubs. Consistent performance. |
| **Aero** | Australia | Quantum, GrooVe, Sonic, Optima, Z Scoop | $500-$650+ | Players wanting customization | Most grip styles and colors of any brand. Advanced manufacturing. 12-week delivery due to global demand. |

**Key Insight:** Bowls are NOT commodity products. They require fitting -- players need specific sizes (000 to 5), weights (medium/heavy), and bias profiles for their position and playing conditions. This makes traditional dropshipping very difficult for bowls themselves.

**Sizing Reality:**
- Men typically use sizes 3-5
- Women typically use sizes 00-2
- The only way to find your size is to physically hold different bowls
- Clubs usually have "try before you buy" sets

**Dropshipping Verdict for Bowls: DIFFICULT.** High price point ($400-650/set), sizing requirements, heavy weight (~6.5kg per set of 4), limited suppliers, and 4-12 week custom order times make true dropshipping impractical. Better approach: affiliate links to authorized dealers (Henselite USA, Aero Bowls USA, Bowls America).

### 1.5 Recommended Ecommerce Strategy

**DO NOT try to be a full dropshipping lawn bowls retailer.** The market is too small, products too specialized, and margins too thin on the big-ticket items.

**Instead, build a HYBRID model:**

1. **Accessories Direct Sale** -- Source from Alibaba/wholesale for measures, chalk, polish, cloths, jacks. Bundle into "starter kits." Ship from a small inventory or use a 3PL.

2. **Print-on-Demand Club Merchandise** -- Integrate with Printful/Printify for custom club shirts, hats, visors. Tie to the club directory (each club gets a "merchandise" page). This is a genuine differentiator.

3. **Affiliate for Big Ticket** -- Partner with Henselite USA, Aero Bowls USA, Bowls America for bowls, shoes, bags. Earn 5-10% commission. Buyer's guide content drives these sales.

4. **Content Monetization** -- Display ads (Mediavine/AdThrive once traffic qualifies) and sponsored content from equipment brands.

5. **Second-Hand Marketplace (Future)** -- There is an active used bowls market (one seller reported 2,700+ sets sold over 16 years on eBay). A dedicated lawn bowls marketplace within Pick-a-Partner could capture this demand.

### 2.3 Competitive Content Analysis

**Jack High Bowls (jackhighbowls.com)** -- The dominant lawn bowls content site:
- 300+ posts covering virtually every lawn bowls topic
- Categories: beginner guides, equipment reviews, tactics, rules, brand comparisons
- Run on GitHub Pages (Jekyll) -- technically simple but content-rich
- Monetized with ads
- Likely generating significant organic traffic in this niche
- **Takeaway:** This is our primary content competitor. We can differentiate by being US-focused (they are UK/Australia-centric), more visual/interactive, and tying content to our club directory and app.

**Other Content Competitors:**
- Bowls.co.uk -- UK focused, comprehensive beginner guide
- HowToPlaySport.com -- multi-sport site with lawn bowls section
- Elakai Outdoor blog -- comparison content (lawn bowling vs bocce vs petanque)
- Wikipedia -- glossary and rules (hard to outrank but can capture featured snippets)
- Various club websites -- thin content, poor SEO, but rank for local terms

**Content Gap (our opportunity):**
1. **No authoritative US-focused lawn bowls content site exists.** Jack High Bowls is the leader but is UK/Australia-oriented.
2. **No site connects content to a functional app** (club directory, tournament brackets, partner matching).
3. **Video content is almost nonexistent** -- YouTube has very few quality lawn bowling tutorials.
4. **Interactive content** (quizzes, "which bowl is right for me" tools, glossary lookups) does not exist.

### 4.6 SEO Technical Recommendations

1. **Schema markup** on all /learn pages (FAQ schema, HowTo schema, Article schema)
2. **Featured snippet optimization** -- Format answers in tables, numbered lists, and direct answer paragraphs
3. **Image SEO** -- Use original images of lawn bowling (not stock photos). Alt text with keywords.
4. **Internal linking density** -- Every page should have 3-5 internal links minimum
5. **URL structure** -- Keep URLs clean: /learn/rules not /learn/lawn-bowling-rules-explained-2026
6. **Content freshness** -- Update buyer's guides annually (append current year to title for freshness signals)
7. **Local SEO** -- Club directory pages should have LocalBusiness schema, city-specific landing pages
8. **Build topical authority** -- Google rewards sites that comprehensively cover a topic. 50+ quality pages on lawn bowling will establish Pick-a-Partner as the authority site.

## 5. Market Size Reality Check

**Honest Assessment:**

- Bowls USA has ~2,800 members across 7 divisions nationally
- There are approximately 200-300 lawn bowling clubs in the USA (many small/aging membership)
- The sport skews heavily toward seniors (60+)
- However, "barefoot bowls" (casual/social bowling) is growing as a social activity among younger demographics in Australia and starting to appear in the US
- The global lawn bowls market is growing, driven by Australia, UK, South Africa, and New Zealand
- The US is an underpenetrated market with growth potential

**What this means for strategy:**
- Do NOT expect ecommerce to be a major revenue driver. The addressable market is too small for volume-based retail.
- Content/SEO is the highest-leverage strategy because the competition is almost nonexistent
- The real value is building the platform (club directory + partner matching + tournament management) and using content to drive adoption
- Ecommerce should be a supplementary revenue stream, not the primary business model
- Focus on accessories, print-on-demand, and affiliate rather than inventory-based retail
