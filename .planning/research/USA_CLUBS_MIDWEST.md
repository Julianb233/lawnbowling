### Key Observations

- The Midwest is a **sparse region** for lawn bowling compared to the coasts. The Central Division of Bowls USA covers this territory and lists only a handful of affiliated clubs.
- **Minnesota stands out** with 4 venues, though 3 of 4 are commercial/municipal rental facilities rather than traditional member-run clubs.
- **Traditional member-run clubs** exist in Milwaukee, Cincinnati, and Chicago (status uncertain for Chicago).
- The **Fort Wayne Lawn Bowls** initiative (Indiana) represents the only new club development in the region, with a groundbreaking in July 2025.
- Seven of twelve Midwest states appear to have **no lawn bowling presence whatsoever**.
- Season is universally **May through October** due to the climate.
- The Central Division historically formed in **1946** as one of seven Bowls USA divisions.

## Implications for Pick-a-Partner

### Market Opportunity

The Midwest represents a **significantly underserved market** for lawn bowling. With only ~9 venues across 12 states (and 7 states having zero presence), there is limited but concentrated activity. Key implications:

1. **Small but engaged user base**: The clubs that do exist (Milwaukee, Cincinnati) have dedicated, long-standing memberships. These are high-value potential users who are passionate about the sport.

2. **Demand signals**: Brit's Pub's 10-year league waitlist and Fort Wayne's new construction suggest latent demand exists but infrastructure doesn't.

3. **Club vs. venue distinction matters**: Traditional member clubs (Milwaukee, Cincinnati) have different needs than commercial venues (Brit's Pub) or municipal facilities (Brookview). The app should accommodate both models.

4. **Technology gap**: Most clubs have basic web presence. Milwaukee is the most digitally advanced with a member portal. There is a clear opportunity for a purpose-built platform.

5. **Seasonal nature**: All Midwest venues operate May-October only. App engagement will be seasonal, which needs to be factored into retention strategies.

6. **Cross-regional connectivity**: With clubs so spread out (250+ miles between some), inter-club tournament coordination and partner-finding across distances becomes valuable.
