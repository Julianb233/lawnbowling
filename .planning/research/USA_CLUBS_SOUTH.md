**Key findings:**
- Florida dominates the Southern US lawn bowling landscape with 13 of 19 identified clubs
- The Southeast Division (SED) of Bowls USA has 12-14 member clubs, almost all in Florida plus 2 in North Carolina
- The South Central Division (SCD) covers Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana (plus AZ, NM, UT) but no specific lawn bowling clubs in the southern states of that division were identified through research
- Arkansas has one independent/small club in Hot Springs Village
- Tennessee has one commercial lawn bowling venue (bar/restaurant) in Nashville, not a traditional club
- No lawn bowling clubs were found in Georgia, South Carolina, Alabama, Mississippi, Kentucky, Louisiana, Oklahoma, or Texas
- Florida's concentration is driven by retirement communities (Sun City Center, The Villages, Kings Point, Maple Leaf, etc.)

**Division coverage for target states:**
- **Southeast Division (SED):** Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee
- **South Central Division (SCD):** Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana (plus Arizona, New Mexico, Utah)
- **Central Division:** Kentucky (plus Midwest states)

## FLORIDA (13 clubs)

Florida is the epicenter of lawn bowling in the Southern US. The state benefits from year-round warm weather and a large retirement community population, many of whom come from Canada, the UK, and northern US states where lawn bowling is popular.

## ANALYSIS & OBSERVATIONS

### Geographic Concentration

The lawn bowling landscape in the Southern US is almost exclusively a **Florida phenomenon**, driven by:
1. **Retirement communities** - The majority of Florida clubs are within 55+ gated communities (Sun City Center, Kings Point, The Villages, Maple Leaf, OTOW/World Parkway, Sun-n-Fun)
2. **Canadian/British snowbird population** - Many members are seasonal residents from Canada and the UK where lawn bowls is popular
3. **Year-round warm climate** - Enables bowling throughout the year
4. **Established infrastructure** - Some clubs date back nearly 100 years (St. Petersburg 1916, Lakeland 1923, Clearwater 1924)

### Club Types

| Type | Examples | Count |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Retirement community (restricted)** | Sun City Center, Kings Point, The Villages, Maple Leaf, OTOW, Sun-n-Fun | 6 |

### Membership Trends

- **Declining at older clubs:** Clearwater went from 250+ members in the 1990s to ~100 now
- **Growing at newer clubs:** The Villages (est. 2014) is growing steadily
- **Seasonal pattern:** Many clubs (especially retirement communities) are most active Nov-Apr
- **International membership:** Canadian, British, Australian, German members common

### Key Gaps and Opportunities

1. **Texas:** With a large population and growing retirement communities, Texas has no confirmed lawn bowling clubs. This represents a significant market gap.
2. **Georgia/Atlanta:** No lawn bowling despite being a major metro. Only bocce ball exists.
3. **Nashville:** Fogg Street proves commercial demand exists; no traditional club.
4. **South Carolina coastal areas:** Hilton Head, Charleston, Myrtle Beach - affluent retirement areas with no lawn bowling.
5. **Technology:** Most clubs have poor-to-mediocre online presence. Only Mount Dora, Clearwater, Sarasota, and Fogg Street have genuinely useful websites. Online registration/booking is rare.
