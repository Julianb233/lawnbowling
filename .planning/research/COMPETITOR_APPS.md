# Competitor Apps Analysis

**Domain:** Lawn bowls tournament management software
**Researched:** 2026-03-10
**Overall confidence:** MEDIUM (niche market, limited public reviews)

## Market Overview

The lawn bowls software market is fragmented, regional, and dominated by UK/Australian vendors serving an aging demographic. Most products are club management suites that include tournament features as one module among many. There is **no dominant player** focused specifically on tournament-day check-in and draw generation -- which is our exact niche.

---

## Tier 1: Full Club Management Suites

### Bowlr (bowlr.co.uk)
**Market position:** Leading UK solution, approved by Bowls England and EIBA
**Users:** 185+ UK bowls clubs

**Core features:**
- Membership management (contacts, fees, lockers)
- Online rink booking with calendar
- League management with automated fixture generation
- Knock-out competition module (4+ teams, random allocation, auto-progression)
- Members score input (self-service results entry)
- Accounts module (rink financials, invoicing)
- Rinkside iPad scoring with auto data entry
- TV screen integration for rink diaries/results
- Automated email notifications
- Cloud-based, accessible anywhere

**What they do well:**
- Most polished, modern solution in the market
- National-level integration (Bowls England competitions portal)
- Rinkside iPad scoring is innovative
- Strong case study: Bedford Borough BC saved a full working day per week
- Accessibility-focused design for wide age demographic

**What they lack:**
- No public mobile app for players (web-only)
- No real-time tournament-day check-in kiosk mode
- No position-based draw engine (skip/vice/second/lead pooling)
- Draw is random allocation only, not skill/position-balanced
- No "pick-a-partner" social matching for social bowls
- Pricing not transparent (quote-based)

**Pricing:** Not publicly disclosed. Quote-based after demo.

**Confidence:** HIGH (verified via official website and case studies)

### BowlsManager (bowlsmanager.com)
**Market position:** UK-focused, established player

**Core features:**
- Web-based membership database management
- Fixtures list and competition management
- Rich reporting suite (performance, contributions, mileage, tea duties)
- Mobile-friendly club website included
- No software installation required

**What they do well:**
- Transparent pricing
- Comprehensive reporting (even tracks tea duties -- very club-culture aware)
- Low training requirement for novice users

**What they lack:**
- No tournament-day operations (check-in, draw, scoring)
- No real-time features
- No mobile app
- UK-only focus

**Pricing:** GBP 415/year + GBP 175 one-time website setup

**Confidence:** MEDIUM (pricing verified, features from marketing copy)

---

### Bowls Hub (bowls-hub.co.uk)
**Market position:** Newer UK entrant, aggressive pricing

**Core features:**
- Round-robin fixture generation with fair rink allocation
- Player availability tracking (one-click for players)
- Auto-open/auto-close scheduling
- Reserve player management
- Match scoring and results tracking
- Automatic standings/league tables
- Rink booking with interactive calendar
- Membership management with Stripe payments
- iOS and Android mobile apps
- Push notifications for fixtures and team updates
- Player stats (games played, win/loss, home/away, attendance)

**What they do well:**
- Only competitor with native mobile apps (iOS + Android)
- All-inclusive pricing (no module upselling)
- Modern tech stack
- Push notifications
- First year free

**What they lack:**
- No tournament-day draw engine
- No check-in kiosk mode
- No social bowls / pick-a-partner functionality
- Newer product, smaller user base

**Pricing:**
| Plan | Annual Cost | Member Limit |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Small Club | GBP 150 | 50 members |
| Medium Club | GBP 250 | 100 members |
| Large Club | GBP 400 | 200 members |
| Custom | Bespoke | 200+ |

First year free on all plans.

**Confidence:** HIGH (pricing and features verified from official website)

## Tier 2: Tournament-Specific Tools

### PAMI (pamibowls.com)
**Market position:** Australia/New Zealand focused, tournament scoring specialist since 2011

**Core features:**
- Tournament results calculation
- Section play (players change teams each round)
- A Sides (six-player team format)
- Social bowls scoring
- Sets play (4-round and up to 9-game configs)
- Score entry in any order (no scrolling)
- Error alerts on entry
- Section and overall rankings
- Configurable win/draw points

**What they do well:**
- Purpose-built for tournament day operations
- Fast score entry workflow
- Handles multiple tournament formats
- 15+ years of refinement

**What they lack:**
- No check-in / draw generation
- No real-time display
- No web/mobile -- appears to be desktop software
- No team formation / position-based draws
- Dated interface
- Australia/NZ focused

**Pricing:** Not publicly disclosed

**Confidence:** MEDIUM (features from official site, no hands-on verification)

---

**What they do well:**

**What they lack:**

---

### GitHub LawnBowls Tool (iljohnson/LawnBowls)
**Market position:** Open-source utility

**Core features:**
- Python/Windows GUI tool
- Random draw given teams, rinks, and rounds
- Round-robin format with best-fit algorithm
- Rules: no team plays same rink twice, no same matchup twice
- Different output each run (shuffled)

**What they do well:**
- Algorithmic approach to fair draw generation
- Open source, inspectable logic

**What they lack:**
- CLI/desktop only
- No web interface
- No check-in
- No scoring

**Confidence:** HIGH (source code available)

## Key Takeaway

**Nobody does what Pick-a-Partner does.** The market splits into:

1. **Club management suites** (Bowlr, BowlsManager, Bowls Hub) -- focused on season-long membership, leagues, and admin. Tournament day is an afterthought.

2. **Tournament calculators** (PAMI, Drawmaker) -- focused on scoring/results after games are played. No check-in or draw workflow.

3. **Individual score trackers** (rollUp, Bowls Score Card) -- personal tools, no tournament operations.

**The gap we fill:** Real-time tournament-day operations -- check-in, position-based draw generation, team formation, rink assignment -- running on a kiosk iPad at the club with a modern mobile-first UI. This is currently done with paper tags, a physical draw board, and a volunteer drawmaster. We are digitizing the drawmaster.
