## 1. Country-by-Country Breakdown

### 1.1 Australia — The Largest Market

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Governing Body | Bowls Australia |
| Registered Members | ~240,000 |
| Social/Casual Participants | ~500,000+ |
| Clubs | ~2,000+ |
| National Technology Platform | BowlsLink (whole-of-sport IT system) |
| Key States | NSW, Victoria, Queensland, SA, WA |
| Sport Status | Major participation sport; cultural institution via RSL/community clubs |

**Key Facts:**
- Bowls Australia conducts an annual National Bowls Census tracking participation at club, state, and national level.
- BowlsLink is the dominant national technology platform, built by Melbourne-based agency Savage Bull. It provides member management, competition management, live scoring, and results — free to clubs.
- The Bowls Premier League (BPL) has increased the sport's media profile.
- The 2024-25 Annual Report shows a return to financial surplus, signaling organizational stability.
- Australia has the most digitally mature bowls ecosystem globally.

**Opportunity:** Largest addressable market but also strongest incumbent (BowlsLink). Any product entering Australia must either integrate with or clearly differentiate from BowlsLink.

### 1.2 United Kingdom

The UK is the second-largest market globally but is fragmented across four separate governing bodies.

#### England

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Governing Body | Bowls England |
| Affiliated Clubs | ~2,000 |
| Estimated Players | ~180,000-200,000 affiliated; 270,000+ total participants |
| Indoor Body | English Indoor Bowling Association (EIBA) |
| Growth Trend | +1.4% affiliated bowlers in 2023; stable/growing in 2024 |

**Key Facts:**
- Bowls England runs major participation initiatives: Bowls' Big Weekend (660+ clubs in 2024), Bowls Bash, and PlayBowls.
- Insurance provided through Sutton Winson (Civil Liability PS5M, Employers' Liability PS10M).
- Over 8,000 bowling clubs exist across the entire UK (including crown green bowls).
- Indoor bowls is a separate but significant segment (EIBA).

#### Scotland

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Governing Body | Bowls Scotland |
| Members | ~49,500 |
| Clubs | ~900+ (including ~180 in Perth & Kinross to Orkney region alone) |
| Growth Trend | -1% overall but +40% junior membership growth |
| National Centre | Northfield, Ayr |

**Key Facts:**
- Over 1,000 players compete in National Championships annually.
- Strong regional club structure with county-level competition.
- "Try Bowls" program delivered 2% membership growth in participating clubs with 494 new members.

#### Wales

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Governing Body (Umbrella) | Welsh Bowls Federation |
| Men's Outdoor Body | Welsh Bowling Association (est. 1904) |
| Total Affiliated Clubs (all forms) | ~670 |
| Total Members | ~25,000+ |
| Outdoor Men's Clubs | 286 across 10 counties |
| Indoor Clubs | 24 (Welsh Indoor Bowls) |

#### Northern Ireland & Ireland

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Governing Body | Irish Bowling Association (IBA) |
| Total IBA Clubs | 121 (95 in NI, 28 in Republic) |
| Sub-Associations | NIPGL, NIBA, NIPBA, Bowling League of Ireland |
| Republic Concentration | Dublin area and Letterkenny |

**UK Opportunity:** Fragmented governance = fragmented technology. No single platform dominates like BowlsLink does in Australia. This is a significant gap and opportunity.

## 4. Terminology Differences

Understanding regional terminology is critical for localization. Users in different countries will search for and expect different terms.

### Sport Name

| Region | Primary Term | Also Acceptable | Never Use |
|--------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| Australia | "Bowls" or "Lawn Bowls" | "Outdoor Bowls" | "Lawn Bowling" (sounds American) |
| UK | "Bowls" | "Lawn Bowls", "Flat Green Bowls" | "Lawn Bowling" |
| NZ | "Bowls" | "Lawn Bowls" | "Lawn Bowling" |
| South Africa | "Bowls" | "Lawn Bowls" | "Lawn Bowling" |
| Canada | "Lawn Bowls" | "Bowls", "Boulingrin" (French) | — |
| USA | "Lawn Bowling" or "Lawn Bowls" | "Bowls" | — |
| Hong Kong | "Lawn Bowls" | "Bowls" | — |

### Key Terms

| Concept | Australia/NZ | UK | USA | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-----|------|-------|
| Target ball | Jack | Jack | Jack | Universal primary term |
| Target ball (alt.) | Kitty, Cot, Pill, Dolly, White | Kitty, Cot | Kitty | Australia has the most variants |
| Playing piece | Bowl (or "wood") | Bowl | Bowl | "Wood" is legacy term from when bowls were wooden |
| A round of play | End | End | End | Universal |
| Playing area | Rink | Rink | Rink | Universal |
| Playing surface | Green | Green | Green | Universal |
| Boundary marker | Ditch | Ditch | Ditch | Universal |
| Delivery mat | Mat | Mat | Mat | Universal |
| Crown green bowls | N/A | Crown Green | N/A | UK-only variant (mainly Northern England) |
| Short mat bowls | N/A | Short Mat | N/A | UK indoor variant |
| Team of 4 | Fours / Rink | Fours / Rink | Fours | — |
| Team of 3 | Triples | Triples | Triples | — |
| Team of 2 | Pairs | Pairs | Pairs / Doubles | USA sometimes uses "Doubles" |
| Solo game | Singles | Singles | Singles | — |
| Curve of the bowl | Bias | Bias | Bias | Universal |
| Close to the jack | Shot / Toucher | Shot / Toucher | Shot | — |
| Casual game | Roll-up | Roll-up | Open Play | — |

### Localization Implications

- **Product UI must use "Bowls"** as the default sport name, not "Lawn Bowling" (except for US-specific pages).
- **Search/filter terms** should include all regional variants (jack, kitty, cot, pill, dolly).
- **Crown Green Bowls** is a distinct variant played mainly in Northern England and should be treated as a separate category if expanding into UK market.
- **French localization** required for Canadian market ("Boulingrin").

## 5. Technology Gaps & Competitors

### Current Technology Landscape

| Platform | Country | Type | Coverage | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|----------|---------|------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **BowlsLink** | Australia | Whole-of-sport platform | National (all states) | Member mgmt, competitions, live scoring, free to clubs | Australia-only; built by single agency (Savage Bull) |
| **BowlsHub** | New Zealand | Competition results | National | Results portal at all levels | Less comprehensive than BowlsLink |
| **BowlsManager** | UK (primarily) | Club management | Individual clubs | Web-based club mgmt | Not federated / no national integration |
| **Lawn Bowling Club Manager** | USA/International | Club websites + mgmt | Individual clubs | Member registration, leagues | Small scale |
| **Playpass** | International | Sports management (general) | Multi-sport | Free tier, tournament/league mgmt | Not bowls-specific |
| **PAMI Bowls** | International | Tournament software | Individual clubs | Tournament result calculation | Narrow feature set |
| **rollUp Scorecard** | Australia | Scoring app | Individual matches | Free, simple | Limited scope |
| **Bowls Score Card** | UK/International | Mobile scoring | Individual matches | QR code competition setup | Basic functionality |
| **Kazo Vision** | International | AI-powered scoring | Facilities with cameras | Automated visual scoring | Expensive; requires hardware |

### Technology Gaps by Country

| Country | Member Mgmt | Competition Mgmt | Live Scoring | Club Websites | Analytics | Gap Level |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|---------------|-----------|-----------|
| Australia | BowlsLink | BowlsLink | BowlsLink | Mixed | Low | **LOW** (saturated) |
| England | Fragmented | Fragmented | Minimal | Individual | None | **HIGH** |
| Scotland | Basic | Basic | Minimal | Individual | None | **HIGH** |
| Wales | Basic | Basic | None | Individual | None | **VERY HIGH** |
| NZ | BowlsHub (partial) | BowlsHub | Limited | Mixed | None | **MEDIUM** |
| South Africa | None/manual | None/manual | None | Individual | None | **VERY HIGH** |
| Canada | None/manual | None/manual | None | Individual | None | **VERY HIGH** |
| Hong Kong | Basic | Basic | Some | Club-based | None | **HIGH** |
| Singapore | None | None | None | Club websites | None | **VERY HIGH** |
| USA | None/manual | None/manual | None | Individual | None | **VERY HIGH** |

### Key Insight

**Outside of Australia (and partially NZ), there is no integrated, whole-of-sport technology platform.** The UK — the second-largest market — has no equivalent of BowlsLink. This represents the single biggest technology gap and commercial opportunity.

## 7. Growth Strategy

### Expansion Priority Tiers

#### Tier 1 — Enter First (Lowest Hanging Fruit)

| Country | Why | Effort | Expected Payoff |
|---------|-----|--------|-----------------|
| **England** | Largest underserved market; no BowlsLink equivalent; 2,000 clubs; Bowls England actively seeking digital solutions; English-speaking | Medium | **Very High** |
| **Scotland** | Adjacent to England; separate NGB but similar needs; 900+ clubs | Low (if England done) | High |

**Strategy for UK Entry:**
1. Partner with Bowls England and Bowls Scotland directly — offer pilot program.
2. Localize terminology ("bowls" not "lawn bowling").
3. Integrate with existing insurance schemes (Sutton Winson).
4. Target Bowls' Big Weekend as a launch vehicle (660+ participating clubs = instant distribution).
5. Support Crown Green Bowls as a separate mode to capture Northern England.

| **Ireland (all-island)** | UK adjacency; 121 clubs | Low (if UK done) | Low-Medium |
| **South Africa** | 23K players, 513 clubs; English-speaking; zero tech solutions | Medium | Medium |

#### Tier 3 — Longer Term

## Appendix B: Strategic Recommendations Summary

1. **Enter the UK first.** England + Scotland represent ~250K+ players across ~3,000 clubs with no integrated technology platform. This is the single largest addressable gap in the global market.

2. **Partner with national federations.** Bowls England and Bowls Scotland are actively investing in participation growth (Bowls' Big Weekend, Try Bowls). A technology partnership aligns with their strategic goals.

3. **Do not compete head-on with BowlsLink in Australia** — instead, either integrate (API/data partnerships) or focus on features BowlsLink lacks (analytics, social features, cross-club discovery).

4. **Use "Bowls" as the default product name globally.** Only use "Lawn Bowling" for US-specific marketing.

5. **Build for federation-level partnerships.** The sport's governance structure (national bodies > state/county > clubs) means that a single federation deal can unlock hundreds of clubs simultaneously.

6. **Prioritize English-speaking Commonwealth nations** for the first 18 months (UK, NZ, SA, Canada), then expand into Asia (HK, Singapore) as a beachhead for the emerging Chinese market.

7. **Secure country-code domains** (.co.uk, .com.au, .co.nz, .co.za, .ca) before announcing international expansion.
