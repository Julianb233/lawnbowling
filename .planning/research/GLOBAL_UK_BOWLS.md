## 1. Market Overview

### Total Market Size

| Metric | England | Scotland | UK Total |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| Affiliated Clubs | ~2,700 (flat green) | ~816 | ~3,500+ |
| Registered Players | ~100,000 | ~49,458 | ~150,000 |
| County/District Associations | 35 counties | 32 districts | 67 |
| Crown Green Clubs (England) | Additional ~2,000+ (BCGBA) | N/A | ~2,000+ |
| Crown Green Players | ~70,000+ (BCGBA) | N/A | ~70,000+ |

**Note:** The total UK bowls market encompasses both flat green (lawn bowls) and crown green bowls. Crown green is predominantly played in Northern England and Midlands. Our primary target is flat green clubs, but crown green represents an expansion opportunity.

### Key Market Characteristics

- **Demographic:** Predominantly 55+ age group, but active recruitment of younger players
- **Seasonal:** Outdoor season April–September; many clubs also have indoor facilities for winter play
- **Structure:** Clubs are affiliated to county associations, which affiliate to Bowls England/Scotland
- **Trend:** Membership has been broadly stable with pockets of growth; Bowls England reports "stable affiliation figures" at 2025 AGM
- **Growth Drivers:**
  - Glasgow 2026 Commonwealth Games (bowls and para bowls included)
  - Bowls' Big Weekend (800+ clubs participated in 2025 — record)
  - Bowls Development Alliance actively working to grow participation
  - 30% growth in disability bowls player numbers
  - 40% rise in junior membership in Scotland
  - Increasing focus on inclusivity and accessibility

## 4. Technology Gap Analysis

### Current State of Club Technology in the UK

The UK bowls market is in **early-to-mid digital adoption**. Most clubs still rely on manual processes for core administration, but awareness of digital tools is growing rapidly.

#### Estimated Technology Usage

| Method | Estimated % of Clubs | Notes |
|--------|----------------------|-------|
| Paper & spreadsheets | ~60–65% | Fixtures in notebooks, membership on Excel/paper, phone calls for availability |
| Basic website only | ~20% | WordPress/Wix site for news, no management functionality |
| Dedicated bowls software | ~10–15% | Using Bowlr, BowlsManager, Bowls-Hub, etc. |
| Full digital management | ~5% | Integrated online booking, payments, communications |

#### Existing Competitors

| Platform | Est. Users | Pricing | Key Features | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|----------|-----------|---------|--------------|-----------|------------|
| **Bowlr** | 200+ clubs, 60,000+ members | From £8/month (paid annually) | Membership, bookings, leagues, accounts, competitions | UK market leader; approved by EIBA and Bowls England; modular system | Modular pricing can add up; UI could be more modern |
| **Bowls-Hub** | Unknown (newer) | Single transparent price, all features included | Match management, fixtures, rink booking, Stripe payments, GDPR compliance | All 100+ features for one price; modern; UK-built; enterprise-grade security | Newer entrant, smaller user base |
| **BowlsManager** | Unknown | £415/year | Membership database, fixtures, competitions | Established; web-based | Higher annual cost; less modern |

#### Key Gaps in Current Solutions

1. **No integrated scoring/live results** — No platform offers real-time match scoring that feeds into league tables automatically
2. **No mobile-first design** — Most solutions are web-based with dated mobile experiences
3. **Poor member engagement** — No platform offers push notifications, social features, or community engagement tools
4. **No green/weather integration** — No system connects weather data with green maintenance scheduling
5. **Limited analytics** — No performance tracking, player statistics, or club health dashboards
6. **No cross-club features** — No platform enables inter-club fixture management, touring arrangements, or cross-county coordination
7. **No TV scoreboard / spectator features** — No system offers public-facing scoreboard displays
8. **No integrated e-commerce** — No club management system includes equipment shop functionality
9. **No onboarding/coaching tools** — Nothing for new member onboarding, coaching programs, or skill development tracking
10. **Fragmented landscape** — Clubs use separate tools for website, email, payments, and management

## 6. Market Entry Strategy

### Recommended Approach: "Land and Expand via Governing Bodies"

#### Phase 1: Validation & Pilot (Months 1–3)

1. **Localize the platform** for UK market:
   - British English terminology (green, rink, wood, jack, end, etc.)
   - GBP currency, UK date formats, VAT handling
   - UK-specific league formats (county leagues, inter-club, Top Club competitions)
   - GDPR compliance (already required but verify full compliance)

2. **Secure 5–10 pilot clubs** across England and Scotland:
   - Target clubs with existing websites (digitally aware)
   - Prioritize mixed indoor/outdoor clubs (year-round usage)
   - Include 1–2 from each region: London, South, Midlands, North, Scotland
   - Offer completely free for 12 months

3. **Build UK-specific features:**
   - Bowls England competition structure integration
   - County league reporting format
   - Green maintenance & weather integration (UK-specific weather data)
   - Rink booking system (critical for UK clubs)

#### Phase 2: Governing Body Endorsement (Months 3–6)

1. **Approach Bowls England** with pilot results and case studies
   - Primary contact: Tom Green (Marketing & Partnerships) or Jon Cockcroft (CEO)
   - Propose "Official Digital Partner" status
   - Offer preferential pricing for all affiliated clubs

2. **Approach Bowls Scotland** simultaneously
   - Leverage Glasgow 2026 Commonwealth Games momentum
   - Propose technology showcase at Games-related events

3. **Partner with BDA** for club development integration
   - BDA's 7 development officers can champion the platform
   - Integrate with their Club Development Programme

#### Phase 3: County-Level Rollout (Months 6–12)

1. **Target 5 high-priority counties:**
   - Surrey (128 clubs, 6,625 members)
   - Hampshire (major county, strong clubs)
   - Devon (large, enthusiastic county)
   - Yorkshire (major northern county)
   - Essex (large metropolitan county)

2. **County ambassador program:**
   - Train 1–2 tech-savvy members per county as champions
   - Free premium accounts for county association officers
   - County-level dashboards for association management

3. **Bowls' Big Weekend integration:**
   - Offer free sign-up/registration tools for the 800+ participating clubs
   - Convert Big Weekend participants into platform users

#### Phase 4: Scale (Months 12–24)

1. **Pricing Strategy:**
   - Free tier: Basic club website + member directory (converts from spreadsheets)
   - Standard: £5–8/month (undercut Bowlr at £8/month) — league management, fixtures, booking
   - Premium: £12–15/month — full suite including payments, analytics, communications
   - County/National bulk pricing

2. **UK-specific marketing:**
   - Advertise in Bowls International magazine
   - Sponsor county events and competitions
   - SEO targeting UK bowls-related keywords
   - Partner with bowlsclub.info (UK bowls website directory)
   - Social media targeting (Facebook is primary channel for UK bowls community)

3. **Differentiation from Bowlr and competitors:**
   - Mobile-first design (biggest gap in market)
   - Live scoring and TV scoreboard features (unique)
   - Push notifications for member engagement (unique)
   - Weather integration for green maintenance (unique)
   - Integrated equipment shop (unique)
   - Free tier to lower adoption barrier (most competitors are paid-only)
   - Modern, beautiful UI vs. functional-but-dated competitors

### Key Success Factors

1. **Bowls England endorsement** is the single most important factor — it instantly legitimizes the platform for 2,700 clubs
2. **Free tier** is essential — many UK clubs are volunteer-run with tiny budgets
3. **Mobile-first** approach differentiates from every existing competitor
4. **Glasgow 2026 Commonwealth Games** creates a once-in-a-generation awareness spike for bowls in the UK
5. **Respect the culture** — UK bowls has deep traditions; technology should enhance, not replace, the social experience
6. **GDPR compliance** is non-negotiable for UK market

### Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Bowlr's established market position (200+ clubs) | Free tier + superior mobile experience + unique features (live scoring, TV scoreboard) |
| Club volunteers resistant to change | Extensive onboarding support; "digital champions" program per county |
| Seasonal usage (outdoor-only clubs) | Target indoor/outdoor clubs first; offer year-round value via social features |
| Brexit-related compliance | Ensure full UK data residency; GDPR compliance |
| Currency/pricing sensitivity | GBP pricing; competitive with Bowlr's £8/month |
| Crowded competitor landscape | Focus on what nobody else offers: mobile-first, live scoring, spectator features |
