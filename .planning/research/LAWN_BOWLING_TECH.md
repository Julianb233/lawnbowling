## 1. Existing Software Landscape

### 1.1 National Federation Systems (Government-backed)

#### BowlsLink (Australia) -- The Market Leader
- **Website:** https://bowls.com.au/club-support/bowlslink-2/
- **Developer:** Memberpoint Pty Ltd, backed by Bowls Australia
- **What it is:** The first purpose-built results and competition management system for lawn bowls in Australia. It is the official national platform mandated/recommended by Bowls Australia.
- **Key Features:**
  - Membership database with player profiles
  - Competition management from basic club events to the Australian Open
  - Online event entry and registration
  - Email campaigns and communications
  - Online payments
  - Free club website with template and custom domain
  - Digital membership cards and certifications
  - Competition draws and results
- **BowlsLink Scoring App** (iOS 14+ and Android 5.2+):
  - Digital scorecard replacing paper
  - End-by-end or total match score entry
  - Two scoring modes: Marker (official) and Competitor (player self-score)
  - Live results submitted directly to BowlsLink portal
  - Scores visible in real-time on the results portal
- **Formerly called:** BowlsNow (rebranded October 2025 to BowlsLink App)
- **Strengths:** Deep integration with national federation, mandatory for many competitions, comprehensive membership database
- **Weaknesses:** Australia-only, designed for competitive/pennant bowls not social/pick-a-partner formats, bureaucratic interface, not designed for day-of tournament check-in
- **Confidence:** HIGH (official Bowls Australia source)

#### BowlsHub Aotearoa (New Zealand)
- **Website:** https://bowlsnewzealand.co.nz/hubs/bowlshub/
- **Developer:** Bowls New Zealand
- **What it is:** National centralized competition management and membership database for all of New Zealand lawn bowls.
- **Key Features:**
  - Membership management across categories (Full, Limited, Social)
  - Membership fee invoicing and payments
  - Competition draws and results input
  - Searchable results archive (club, centre, national levels)
  - Umpire, greenkeeper, and coach certification tracking
  - Player self-service: update profile, pay fees, enter competitions, view results
- **Strengths:** National-level integration, member self-service portal
- **Weaknesses:** NZ-only, focused on administration not day-of-play experience, no real-time scoring
- **Confidence:** HIGH (official Bowls New Zealand source)

#### Bowls South Africa
- **Website:** https://www.bowlssa.co.za/
- **What it is:** The governing body runs traditional administration. No purpose-built national software platform was found in research.
- **Current state:** Clubs appear to use generic solutions (spreadsheets, BowlsManager, PlayPass) or manual methods. No equivalent to BowlsLink exists for South Africa.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (no specific SA software found; absence confirmed across multiple searches)

## 2. What Clubs Currently Use

### 2.1 The Reality: Overwhelmingly Manual Processes

Based on research across multiple sources, the vast majority of lawn bowls clubs worldwide still operate with manual or semi-manual systems:

#### The Typical Club Day (Social/Tournament)

1. **Arrival & Check-in:** Players arrive and write their name on a whiteboard, clipboard, or put a numbered chip/marble into a container ("the red can"). No digital registration.

2. **Position Declaration:** Players verbally tell the tournament organizer their preferred position (Skip, Vice/Second, Lead) or it is assumed based on experience. This is tracked mentally or on paper.

3. **Draw Generation:** The tournament convener (usually a volunteer) manually draws teams:
   - **Marble/chip draw:** Numbered tokens drawn randomly from a bag to form teams
   - **Whiteboard method:** Names written on a board, manually assigned to rinks
   - **Pre-printed spreadsheets:** Excel templates printed out, names handwritten in
   - **Drawmaker software:** A few tech-savvy clubs use desktop software to generate draws, then print results

4. **Rink Assignment:** Convener assigns teams to numbered rinks (greens), writes on whiteboard or paper posted on a wall.

5. **Scoring:** Paper scorecards on each rink. Vice-skip keeps score with pen and paper. Results brought to convener at end of game.

6. **Results Calculation:** Convener manually tallies results, or enters into PAMI/spreadsheet. Results written on whiteboard or announced verbally.

7. **Communication:** Results posted on physical notice board at club, sometimes later entered into website or emailed.

## 3. The Technology Gap

### 3.1 What Does NOT Exist (Confirmed Gaps)

After surveying every identifiable product in the lawn bowls software market, the following capabilities are confirmed as absent or severely underserved:

#### GAP 1: Real-Time Tournament Check-In on iPad/Kiosk
- **Current state:** NO product offers a self-service kiosk/iPad check-in for lawn bowls tournaments
- **What exists:** Bowlr has "rinkside iPad scoring" but this is for score entry during a match, not player arrival and check-in
- **The problem:** Players arrive over a 30-minute window. A volunteer with a clipboard tracks who has arrived. Latecomers cause draw delays. No-shows aren't detected until the draw is being made.
- **Confidence:** HIGH -- searched extensively for "lawn bowls kiosk," "lawn bowls check-in," "lawn bowls self-service" with zero results

#### GAP 2: Position Preference Selection (Skip/Lead/Vice)
- **Current state:** NO software found that captures player position preferences during check-in and uses them in draw generation
- **What exists:** Players verbally declare or are assumed to prefer certain positions. The convener keeps this in their head or on paper.
- **The problem:** Many clubs have too many skips and not enough leads. Balanced teams require knowing who wants to play where. Currently this is entirely manual/tribal knowledge.
- **Confidence:** HIGH -- no product mentions position preference in any feature list

#### GAP 3: Automatic Draw Generation Based on Positions
- **Current state:** Draw software exists (Drawmaker, PAMI, GitHub tools) but NONE factor in position preferences
- **What exists:** Random draw generators that ensure rink rotation and avoid repeat matchups. They treat all players as interchangeable.
- **The problem:** A random draw might put three skips on one team and three leads on another. The convener must manually adjust, which is time-consuming and creates complaints about favoritism.
- **Confidence:** HIGH -- verified across PAMI, Drawmaker, PlayPass, and GitHub tools

#### GAP 4: Live Scoring Visible to ALL Players
- **Current state:** LIMITED -- rollUp Live (premium) allows sharing scores via match ID. BowlsLink Scoring shows results on the results portal. GLB claims live leaderboards.
- **What exists partially:** rollUp Live requires each player to have the app and share a match ID. BowlsLink is pennant-focused. GLB is new and adoption unclear.
- **The GAP specifically:** No system where a club puts up a screen (TV, projector, iPad) showing ALL current matches' live scores for spectators and waiting players. The closest is Bowlr's TV screen integration, but that shows fixtures/diaries, not live in-game scores.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM -- GLB may fill this gap eventually, but current real-world adoption is minimal

#### GAP 5: Modern Mobile-First Interface for Elderly Users
- **Current state:** NONE of the bowls-specific software is mobile-first with elderly UX considerations
- **What exists:**
  - BowlsLink app exists but is a traditional database-driven interface
  - rollUp is mobile but basic
  - Bowlr is "cloud-based" but desktop-designed
  - BowlsManager is "mobile-friendly" but not mobile-first
- **The problem:** The average lawn bowls player is 65+. They need:
  - Large touch targets (minimum 48px, preferably 60px+)
  - High contrast text (WCAG AAA)
  - Simple, linear workflows (no complex navigation)
  - Minimal text input (tap to select, not type)
  - Clear visual feedback (am I checked in? what rink am I on?)
  - Works without downloading an app (PWA)
- **Confidence:** HIGH -- verified across all product screenshots and descriptions

#### GAP 6: Integrated End-to-End Social Bowls Workflow
- **Current state:** NO product covers the full social bowls workflow: Check-in --> Position preference --> Draw generation --> Rink assignment --> Live scoring --> Results --> History
- **What exists:** Fragments:
  - BowlsLink: membership + competition results
  - PAMI: results calculation
  - Drawmaker: draw generation
  - rollUp: scoring
  - Paper: everything else
- **The problem:** A convener running a Wednesday social bowls day needs 3-4 different tools/systems plus paper/whiteboard. Nothing connects check-in to draw to scoring to results.
- **Confidence:** HIGH

## 4. Digital Adoption Barriers

### 4.1 Demographic Reality

Lawn bowls has one of the oldest average player demographics in all of sport:

| Statistic | Approximate Value | Source Confidence |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Average player age | 65-70 years | HIGH (multiple community sources) |
| Players 70+ | 40-50% | MEDIUM |
| Players under 50 | <10% | MEDIUM |
| Players with smartphone | 60-70% | MEDIUM (growing, but many feature-phone users remain) |
| Players comfortable with apps | 30-40% | MEDIUM |

### 4.2 Barrier Categories

#### Barrier 1: Age-Related Physical/Cognitive Challenges
- **Vision:** Smaller screens and small text are genuinely difficult for many players. Presbyopia (age-related farsightedness) affects nearly all players.
- **Motor control:** Small touch targets on phones are hard to hit. Arthritis affects grip and fine motor control.
- **Cognitive load:** Multi-step workflows with branching options cause confusion and abandonment.
- **Memory:** Passwords, PINs, and account creation are genuine barriers. "I forgot my password" is the #1 support issue.
- **Research evidence:** JMIR Aging (2025) systematic review identifies "limited digital literacy and physical and cognitive challenges" as primary barriers to health technology adoption in elderly populations. The same applies to sports technology.
- **Mitigation for Pick-a-Partner:**
  - QR code check-in (already built) eliminates account creation friction
  - iPad kiosk with large UI eliminates small-screen problems
  - Minimal-step workflows (check in --> pick position --> done)
  - No password required for basic check-in (venue device)

#### Barrier 2: Volunteer-Run Committee Resistance
- **The dynamic:** Most bowls clubs are run by volunteer committees of 6-12 retired members. The committee makes technology decisions.
- **Decision process:** Committees meet monthly. Technology decisions require proposal, discussion, vote. This can take 3-6 months for even simple changes.
- **Champion dependency:** Digital adoption almost always requires ONE tech-savvy committee member to champion and support the tool. If that person leaves, adoption collapses.
- **"It works fine as it is":** The marble draw has worked for 50 years. The burden of proof is on the new system, not the old one.
- **Political dynamics:** The current convener/tournament director has status because they "know how to run the draw." Technology that replaces this skill can feel threatening.
- **Mitigation for Pick-a-Partner:**
  - Must work ALONGSIDE existing processes (not replace overnight)
  - Must make the convener's life easier, not eliminate their role
  - Must be demonstrable in 5 minutes at a committee meeting
  - Zero training required for players (kiosk is self-explanatory)
  - Convener gets a powerful admin tool (elevated status, not diminished)

#### Barrier 3: Cost Sensitivity
- **Club economics:** Most lawn bowls clubs operate on thin margins. Annual membership fees are typically AUD 200-500 / GBP 100-300 / USD 150-400.
- **Technology budget:** Most clubs have zero dedicated technology budget. Software competes with green maintenance, equipment, and social events.
- **Reference points:**
  - BowlsManager: GBP 415/year -- considered "less than a new set of bowls" but still debated
  - Drawmaker: AUD 145 one-time -- very affordable
  - PAMI: unknown pricing but used by many clubs suggesting reasonable
  - Bowlr: quote-based, suggesting higher pricing
- **Free alternatives exist:** Excel templates, community spreadsheets, and manual processes are free
- **Mitigation for Pick-a-Partner:**
  - Freemium model essential (basic features free, premium for advanced)
  - Price must be comparable to BowlsManager (~$400-500/year) maximum for full features
  - One-time iPad/kiosk hardware is the real cost barrier (AUD 500-800 for an iPad)
  - ROI must be clear: "saves 30 minutes per tournament" = "saves 2 hours per month of volunteer time"

#### Barrier 4: Lack of Tech Support
- **The problem:** When software breaks at 9am on a Saturday tournament morning, who fixes it?
- **Current state:** Most bowls software has limited support hours. Bowlr offers Mon-Fri 9-5 with weekends optional. Most others have email-only support.
- **The reality:** If the software fails, the club MUST be able to fall back to manual processes immediately. A tournament with 32 players cannot wait for a support ticket.
- **Mitigation for Pick-a-Partner:**
  - Offline-capable PWA (works without internet after initial load)
  - Graceful degradation (if the system is down, print the draw)
  - Self-service admin tools (no support ticket needed for common tasks)
  - WhatsApp/phone support during tournament hours for premium clubs

#### Barrier 5: Fragmented Market
- **The problem:** There is no single standard platform. Each country has different tools. Each club has different preferences.
- **Result:** Clubs are confused by options and default to what they know (paper/Excel).
- **Mitigation for Pick-a-Partner:**
  - Position as "the one tool for tournament day" -- complement, don't replace, existing membership systems
  - Integrate with BowlsLink (Australia) via data export if possible
  - Be sport-agnostic enough to work for lawn bowls, pickleball, tennis (broader market)

## 5. What Would Make Clubs Switch

### 5.1 Killer Features (In Priority Order)

Based on research into what clubs struggle with most and what drives digital adoption:

#### 1. THE DRAW (Highest Value)
**The single most painful point in lawn bowls club operations.**

- Making a fair draw that respects positions, avoids repeat matchups, balances skill levels, and handles odd player counts is HARD
- Currently takes 10-20 minutes of manual work per round
- Subject to complaints about favoritism
- The person who does it is irreplaceable (club depends on their knowledge)

**Killer feature:** Tap "Generate Draw" and get a balanced, fair, position-aware draw in 3 seconds. Display it on a screen everyone can see. Allow manual adjustments. Print if needed.

**This alone would drive adoption.** Every club struggles with this. Every convener would love this.

#### 2. INSTANT CHECK-IN (High Value)
- Eliminates the "who's here?" problem
- No more shouting names across the car park
- Convener knows exactly who has arrived, their position preference, and when
- Handles late arrivals gracefully (add to next round)
- Handles no-shows automatically

**Killer feature:** Player walks up to kiosk, taps their name (or scans QR), selects position preference, done. Convener's screen updates in real-time.

#### 3. VISIBLE RESULTS (High Value)
- Players LOVE seeing results on a big screen
- Currently they cluster around a whiteboard or wait for verbal announcement
- Live scores during play create excitement and engagement

**Killer feature:** Large-format display showing current scores, standings, and upcoming rinks. Updated in real-time as scores are entered.

#### 4. ZERO TRAINING REQUIRED (Essential Enabler)
- If ANY player needs training, adoption will fail
- The kiosk must be as obvious as an ATM
- One-touch interactions only
- "My 85-year-old mother could use it" is the bar

#### 5. ELIMINATION OF CASH HANDLING (Moderate Value)
- Most tournaments have a small entry fee (AUD 5-15)
- Currently collected in cash by a volunteer
- Digital payment eliminates counting, reconciliation, and the risk of "the money box"
- However: MANY elderly players are cash-only, so this must be optional

#### 6. HISTORY AND STATISTICS (Moderate Value)
- "How did I play last month?" -- currently unknowable without personal records
- Club records are in filing cabinets or lost Excel files
- Player stats, win rates, and tournament history are valued by competitive players

#### 7. COMMUNICATION (Lower Value, Already Solved)
- Most clubs already use email and Facebook groups
- Not a differentiator but a nice-to-have if integrated

---

### 5.2 The Adoption Trigger Formula

Research suggests clubs switch when:

```
(Pain of current process) x (Visibility of improvement) x (Zero risk of failure)
   > (Cost) + (Change effort) + (Fear of the unknown)
```

**To maximize the left side:**
- Demo at a real tournament (seeing is believing)
- Let the convener try it for ONE social day alongside the manual process
- Show the results screen on the TV and watch players' faces light up

**To minimize the right side:**
- Free trial (at least 1 month, ideally 3)
- Zero upfront cost for software (iPad is the only cost)
- Always allow fallback to manual (print button for everything)
- One volunteer gets 30 minutes of phone setup help

## 6. Implications for Pick-a-Partner

### 6.1 Competitive Positioning

Pick-a-Partner occupies a genuinely unique position in the market:

| Competitor | Focus | Pick-a-Partner Advantage |
|-----------|-------|--------------------------|
| BowlsLink | National federation admin | Day-of-play experience, not bureaucracy |
| PAMI | Results calculation | Full workflow, not just results |
| Drawmaker | Draw generation | Position-aware draws, real-time, mobile |
| Bowlr | Club admin (UK) | Tournament operations, not club admin |
| GLB | Everything for everyone | Focused simplicity vs. feature bloat |
| rollUp | Individual scoring | Club-wide experience, not personal tool |
| Excel/Paper | Free, familiar | Automated, error-free, real-time |

**Pick-a-Partner's unique value proposition:** The ONLY software that handles the complete social bowls day-of-play workflow from check-in through position selection through draw generation through scoring through results -- with a mobile-first elderly-accessible interface on a venue kiosk.

### 6.2 Recommended Strategy

1. **Lead with the Draw Engine.** This is the killer feature. Position-aware, balanced draws that take 3 seconds instead of 15 minutes. This alone is worth the price of admission.

2. **Kiosk check-in is the Trojan horse.** It gets the iPad into the clubhouse. Once it is there for check-in, it stays for everything else.

3. **Don't try to replace BowlsLink/BowlsHub.** These are entrenched national systems. Complement them. "Use BowlsLink for your pennant season. Use Pick-a-Partner for your social bowls days."

4. **Start in Australia.** Largest organized lawn bowls market. BowlsLink gap (social bowls) is well-understood. Cultural readiness is highest.

5. **Multi-sport is the growth play.** Lawn bowls clubs are the beachhead. But the same check-in-draw-play workflow applies to social tennis, pickleball, croquet, and petanque. The product architecture should remain sport-agnostic.

### 6.3 What to Build First (Phase Priorities)

| Priority | Feature | Rationale |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| P0 | Kiosk check-in with position preference | Unique, no competitor has this, solves real pain |
| P0 | Position-aware draw generation | THE killer feature, primary adoption driver |
| P1 | Large-format results display | High visibility, wow factor, drives word-of-mouth |
| P1 | Round-robin tournament management | Core tournament flow already partially built |
| P2 | Live scoring (basic) | Nice to have, rollUp/BowlsLink partially solve this |
| P2 | Player history and stats | Retention feature, not acquisition feature |
| P3 | Online payment integration | Only for mature clubs, most will want cash option |
| P3 | BowlsLink data export | Australian market integration |
