## ANALYSIS AND OBSERVATIONS

### Regional Patterns

1. **California dominates the Western US lawn bowling landscape** with 42 of 57 clubs (74%). The state has the longest history, most favorable climate, and largest established community.

2. **Arizona is the second largest market** with 7 clubs, concentrated entirely in Sun City/Sun City West retirement communities and one RV resort. The South Central Division (covering AZ) is very retirement-community focused.

3. **Pacific Northwest** has a small but growing presence with 5 clubs across Oregon and Washington. Portland and Seattle clubs are the most active with modern websites and community outreach.

4. **Mountain states** are largely absent - Colorado has exactly one club (Washington Park, Denver), Utah has one (SunRiver, St. George), and the remaining mountain states have none.

### Demographic Patterns

- **55+ retirement communities** account for a significant portion of clubs: Sun City AZ (5), Sun City West AZ (1), Leisure World AZ (1), Rossmoor CA, Del Mesa CA, Friendly Valley CA, Oaks North CA, Laguna Woods CA, Casta del Sol CA, Sun City CA, Oakmont CA, SunRiver UT
- **Public/open clubs** tend to be larger and more active: Laguna Beach (376 members), San Francisco (130), Holmby Park (130), Long Beach (100+), San Diego (100+), Berkeley (95)
- **Younger-skewing clubs** are emerging in urban areas: Portland, Seattle, Berkeley, San Francisco, Hermosa Beach

### Key Insights for Pick-a-Partner

1. **Southwest Division is the largest and most active** with ~1,400 members and ~30 clubs in SoCal alone. This is the primary target market.

2. **Most clubs offer free lessons** - this is nearly universal and represents a strong onboarding culture.

3. **Tournament play is active** - SWD, PIMD, and NWD all run regular tournament schedules, creating demand for partner-finding tools.

4. **Mixed format is common** - Singles, pairs, triples, and rinks (fours) are all played, meaning flexible team formation is important.

5. **Private community clubs** may have limited interest in external technology (they already have captive membership).

6. **Public clubs with growing membership** (Berkeley, Portland, Hermosa Beach, Laguna Beach) are likely the most receptive to technology adoption.

7. **Seasonal patterns matter** - Northwest clubs operate spring-fall; Arizona clubs are busiest in winter (snowbirds); California clubs operate year-round.

8. **Inter-club play is significant** - League play between clubs (e.g., SWD South League) and visitation rights create a networked community.
