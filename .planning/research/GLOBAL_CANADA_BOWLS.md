# Canadian Lawn Bowls Market Research

> **Compiled**: March 2026
> **Purpose**: SaaS platform expansion research for the Canadian lawn bowls market

---

## 1. Market Overview

### Key Statistics
- **Total Clubs**: 270+ registered clubs across 10 provinces (Bowls Canada lists 200+ outdoor + indoor clubs)
- **Total Players**: ~18,000 registered lawn bowlers (down from ~24,000 twenty years ago)
- **Governing Body**: Bowls Canada Boulingrin (BCB)
- **Provincial Associations**: 10 provincial associations
- **Seasonal Considerations**: Outdoor season runs May-September (shorter in prairie/eastern provinces); indoor bowls available year-round at select facilities

### Demographics & Trends
- Most bowlers are over 45 years old
- Active push to attract younger players (relaxing dress codes, youth programs)
- Membership declined from 24,000 to ~13,000-18,000 over 20 years
- Recent growth at urban clubs (e.g., Stanley Park LBC grew from 140 to 350 members)
- LGBTQ+ community engagement driving growth at some urban clubs
- Youth Development Programs launched by Bowls Alberta (2021) and other provinces
- "Learn to Bowl" pilot program rolled out to 25 clubs nationally
- TryBowls.ca launched as national club finder and recruitment tool

## 5. Technology Gap Analysis

### Current Technology Landscape

Canadian lawn bowls clubs operate with a significant technology gap. Most clubs rely on:

- **Paper-based systems**: Draw sheets, sign-up boards, printed schedules
- **Basic websites**: Many clubs use Wix, Weebly, Jimdo, or WordPress free tiers
- **Email/phone trees**: For communication and event coordination
- **Spreadsheets**: For membership tracking and tournament management
- **Facebook groups**: Primary social media channel for most clubs

### Existing Software Solutions

| Software | Origin | Focus | Pricing | Canadian Adoption |
|----------|--------|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Lawn Bowling Club Manager | Canada/USA | Club websites, registration, leagues | Unknown (hosted) | Some Canadian clubs |
| Lawn Bowling Manager | Canada | Membership, leagues, results | Unknown | Limited |
| BowlsManager | UK | Membership, fixtures, competitions | £415/year + £175 setup | Very limited in Canada |
| Playpass | International | Tournaments, leagues, registration | Free tier available | Some clubs |
| PAMI Bowls | International | Tournament management | Unknown | Used for some events |
| Bowlr | UK | Club management | Subscription | Not in Canada |
| SportEasy | International | Multi-sport club management | Freemium | Minimal |

### Technology Gaps & Opportunities

1. **No unified national platform**: Each province and club operates independently with no data sharing
2. **No mobile-first solutions**: Most club websites are not optimized for mobile
3. **No real-time scoring**: Paper scorecards remain the norm
4. **No integrated scheduling**: Members call/email to book rinks or sign up for games
5. **No centralized member directory**: Players cannot easily find clubs or connect with other bowlers across provinces
6. **No player statistics tracking**: No systematic way to track individual performance over time
7. **Limited online payment**: Most clubs collect dues by cheque or cash
8. **No weather integration**: Despite weather being critical for outdoor play
9. **No tournament bracket management**: Draw sheets are still done manually at most clubs
10. **No inter-club coordination**: League scheduling between clubs is done via email/phone

### Key Pain Points for Clubs
- Volunteer administrators overwhelmed with manual processes
- Difficulty attracting and retaining younger members who expect digital experiences
- No easy way for new players to find and connect with clubs
- Tournament organization is time-consuming and error-prone
- Collecting membership dues and event fees is cumbersome

## 6. Market Entry Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-6)

#### Target Market
- **Primary**: British Columbia (49 clubs, tech-savvy urban centers, longest outdoor season)
- **Secondary**: Ontario (115+ clubs, largest market by volume)
- **Tertiary**: Alberta (Commonwealth LBC in Edmonton hosts nationals)

#### Key Partnerships
1. **Bowls Canada Boulingrin**: Approach CEO Britany Gordon for national partnership
   - Align with BCB's strategic plan for innovation and technology
   - Offer to power TryBowls.ca club finder functionality
   - Support the "Learn to Bowl" program digitally
2. **Vancouver & District Bowls Association**: 22 clubs, 2,300 bowlers as pilot market
3. **Bowls BC**: Partnership with Harry Carruthers for provincial rollout

#### Pilot Clubs (Recommended)
1. **Stanley Park LBC** (Vancouver) — Largest club, progressive, tech-friendly demographics
2. **Pacific Indoor Bowls Club** (Vancouver) — Year-round operation, 200+ members
3. **Commonwealth LBC** (Edmonton) — Hosts national championships, wheelchair accessible
4. **Nepean LBC** (Ottawa) — Hosts Para Bowls nationals, accessible focus
5. **Kew Beach LBC** (Toronto) — Large urban club, active social presence

### Phase 2: Growth (Months 6-18)

#### Provincial Association Relationships
- Offer free or discounted platform access to provincial associations
- Provide tournament management tools for provincial championships
- Supply membership tracking for provincial registration requirements
- Generate aggregate participation data for Sport Canada funding applications

#### Key Events for Visibility
- 2025 Canadian Championships (Edmonton, August 2025) — Showcase platform
- Provincial championship events (Spring/Summer each year)
- BCB Annual General Meeting (September each year) — Present/demonstrate

#### Competitive Advantages over Existing Solutions
1. **Canadian-built**: Understanding of bilingual requirements (Quebec), short season challenges
2. **Weather integration**: Critical for outdoor bowls scheduling
3. **Mobile-first**: Appeal to younger demographic BCB is targeting
4. **Free tier**: Lower barrier than BowlsManager (£415/year)
5. **Tournament management**: End-to-end bracket/draw/scoring
6. **Inter-club features**: Cross-club scheduling, player transfers, provincial data

### Phase 3: Scale (Months 18-36)

#### National Rollout
- Leverage BCB partnership for national club onboarding
- Support bilingual (EN/FR) interface for Quebec clubs
- Integration with Sport Canada reporting requirements
- Indoor bowls support (year-round revenue)

#### Revenue Opportunities
- **Freemium model**: Basic club features free, premium for tournaments/leagues
- **Provincial association subscriptions**: Annual fee for provincial-level management
- **National federation license**: BCB-level analytics and reporting
- **Equipment shop integration**: Partnership with Canadian bowls retailers
- **Event registration fees**: Small percentage of tournament entry fees

### Key Success Factors
1. **Volunteer-friendly**: Must be simpler than current manual processes
2. **Offline capability**: Not all greens have reliable wifi
3. **Bilingual support**: Required for Quebec and federal funding eligibility
4. **Accessibility**: Para-bowls community is growing; platform must be WCAG compliant
5. **Seasonal flexibility**: Support both outdoor (May-Sept) and indoor (year-round) operations
6. **Community building**: Features that connect bowlers across clubs and provinces

## 8. Summary & Key Takeaways

### Market Size
- **270+ clubs** across Canada with **~18,000 registered players**
- **BC** (49 clubs) and **Ontario** (115+ clubs) represent **60%+ of total market**
- Short outdoor season (May-September) creates urgency for efficient scheduling

### Opportunity Assessment
- **HIGH opportunity**: Fragmented market with no dominant technology platform
- **Receptive governing body**: BCB actively seeking innovation and modernization
- **Growing youth segment**: Organizations pushing to attract younger, tech-savvy players
- **Volunteer-driven**: Clubs desperate for tools to reduce administrative burden
- **National events**: Championships provide natural visibility and demo opportunities

### Risks
- **Seasonal business**: Most clubs only active 5 months/year (except indoor facilities)
- **Price sensitivity**: Volunteer-run clubs have limited budgets
- **Resistance to change**: Older demographic may resist new technology
- **Low density**: Outside BC/Ontario, clubs are sparse and scattered
- **Bilingual requirement**: Quebec requires French-language support

### Recommended Immediate Actions
1. Contact Britany Gordon (CEO, Bowls Canada) for national partnership discussion
2. Reach out to Harry Carruthers (President, Bowls BC) for pilot program
3. Approach Stanley Park LBC as flagship pilot club
4. Attend 2025 Canadian Championships in Edmonton (August 2025)
5. Build weather integration — unique value proposition for outdoor bowls
