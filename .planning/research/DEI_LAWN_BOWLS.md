# Daily Event Insurance (DEI) for Lawn Bowling Clubs

**Researched:** 2026-03-11
**Overall Confidence:** MEDIUM (verified across multiple sources; USA-specific data is limited compared to UK/AU)

## 1. Lawn Bowling Insurance Needs

### Injury Profile: Why Bowlers Need Coverage

Lawn bowls has a specific and well-documented injury profile that makes it an excellent candidate for per-event insurance. Research from Monash University's Accident Research Centre provides the authoritative data:

**Injury Causes (by frequency):**
| Cause | Percentage | Examples |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Falls (trips, slips) | 54% | Slipping on wet greens, tripping on ditches |
| Overexertion (sprains/strains) | 28% | Back strain from bowling action, repetitive motion |
| Struck by bowl | 18% | Bowls hitting feet, shins, or hands |

**Injury Types:**
| Type | Percentage |
|------|-----------|
| Sprains and strains | 35% |
| Fractures | 35% |
| Cuts and bruises | 23% |
| Other | 7% |

**Most Common Injury Sites:** Ankles and wrists (15% each), followed by knees and lower back.

**Critical Demographic Factor:** 92% of participants are over 45 years old. Players aged 55-75 are more likely to suffer overexertion injuries from exacerbation of previous injuries. 76% of injured players are female (despite only 39% of participants being female), indicating women may be at higher risk -- possibly due to bone density and balance factors in older populations.

**Source confidence:** HIGH -- Monash University Accident Research Centre (MUARC Report 138), Victorian Better Health Channel

### Coverage Types Lawn Bowlers Need

Based on the injury profile and existing insurance products in the market:

| Coverage Type | Why Needed | Priority |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| **Personal accident/injury** | Falls are the #1 cause of injury. Fractures account for 35% of injuries. For an elderly population, a hip fracture from a fall on the green can mean $30K-$50K in medical bills | CRITICAL |
| **Activity injury medical** | Sprains, strains, and contusions during play. The bowling action itself causes overexertion injuries, especially in the 55-75 age group | CRITICAL |
| **Emergency transport** | Ambulance costs ($2,500+) for on-green emergencies. Given the age demographic, cardiac events and serious falls requiring transport are a real risk | HIGH |
| **Per-participant liability** | Protects against claims when one player's bowl strikes another player, or when a player is injured due to green conditions | HIGH |
| **AD&D (Accidental Death & Dismemberment)** | Given the 65+ demographic, accidental death during physical activity is a legitimate concern. Families need coverage | HIGH |
| **Club/event liability** | Visitor injuries on the green, spectator incidents during tournaments, food/beverage liability at club events | HIGH |
| **Volunteer/official liability** | Club officers, greenskeepers, tournament directors -- volunteers who could face personal liability | MEDIUM |
| **Equipment coverage** | Quality bowls cost $300-$600 per set, bags $80-$200, shoes $100-$200. Total kit value $500-$1000 per player | LOW (secondary product) |
| **Travel to away matches** | Not a primary DEI product -- better handled by travel insurance | LOW |

### The DEI Sweet Spot

DEI already covers the CRITICAL and HIGH items through its existing product structure:
- Per-Participant Liability
- Activity Injury Medical
- Emergency Transport
- ActiveGuard AD&D

This is a near-perfect fit for lawn bowls. The existing coverage categories map directly to the documented injury patterns. **No new coverage types need to be invented -- the existing DEI product structure covers lawn bowls comprehensively.**

## 2. Existing Lawn Bowling Insurance Landscape

### Association-Bundled Insurance

Most bowls governing bodies include basic insurance with membership, but coverage is minimal and has significant gaps:

**Bowls USA (United States)**
- National governing body with ~2,800 members across 7 divisions
- Membership dues typically bundled into club fees ($25 Bowls USA portion)
- **Insurance details are notably opaque** -- the website does not publicly detail what coverage is included with membership
- This opacity is itself a selling point for DEI: if players don't know what's covered, they likely aren't adequately covered

**Bowls Australia / State Associations**
- BCIB (Bowling Club Insurance Brokers) is the endorsed broker for Bowls Australia
- Bowls Victoria and Bowls WA have dedicated insurance pages
- Coverage is more comprehensive (public liability, personal accident, management liability) but is **club-level, not per-participant**
- Personal accident coverage through MGA: death benefit up to $50,000 (ages 16-85), weekly income up to $500, non-Medicare medical up to $1,000

**Bowls England**
- Civil and Employers Liability Insurance through Sutton Winson
- Available to affiliated clubs and associations
- Does NOT include personal accident coverage for individual players as standard

**Source confidence:** MEDIUM -- based on official association websites and endorsed broker pages

### Third-Party Insurance Providers

| Provider | Market | Coverage | Pricing |
|----------|--------|----------|---------|
| **MGA Insurance Group** | Australia | Property, liability, personal accident, equipment | Not public; endorsed by Bowls Australia; 20+ years experience |
| **Protectivity** | UK | Public liability (up to GBP 10M), professional indemnity (GBP 1M), equipment (GBP 30K) | From GBP 3.62/month |
| **Sutton Winson** | UK | Civil liability, employers' liability for Bowls England affiliates | Not public |
| **BCIB (Domina)** | Australia | Full club package: property, liability, personal accident, management liability, cyber | Not public |
| **Club Insure** | UK | Buildings, contents, liability, personal accident | Not public; 20+ years experience |
| **Sadler Sports** | USA | General liability ($1M-$5M), accident ($25K-$100K), D&O, equipment | Not public; "up to 38% savings" claim |
| **K&K Insurance** | USA | Amateur sports events, per-participant pricing, up to 2,500 participants per event | Not public; per-participant rate model |
| **Assetsure** | UK | Buildings, contents, all-risks, liability | Not public |

**Source confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH -- direct from provider websites

### The Gap DEI Fills

The critical gap in the existing landscape:

1. **Association insurance is club-level, not participant-level.** Bowls USA/England/Australia provide liability coverage for the CLUB, not for individual players during play. If Player A's bowl strikes Player B and causes a fracture, the club's GL policy may not cover Player B's medical costs.

2. **Per-event coverage does not exist.** Every existing provider offers annual policies. There is NO lawn-bowls-specific per-event or per-session insurance product in the market. A visitor who plays once at a tournament day has zero coverage unless they're a club member with the annual policy.

3. **The demographic needs it most.** Elderly players are most likely to be injured, most likely to have expensive medical outcomes, and most likely to be on fixed incomes where a surprise $15K medical bill is devastating.

4. **Tournament visitors are completely uncovered.** Inter-club competitions, open days, and tournament events bring in non-members who have zero coverage through the host club's policies.

**This is the core pitch: DEI provides the ONLY per-event, per-participant insurance product for lawn bowlers. Nobody else offers this.**

## 3. DEI Product Design for Lawn Bowls

### Pricing Context: What Bowlers Currently Pay

US lawn bowling club membership fees (verified from club websites):
| Club | Annual Fee |
|------|-----------|
| San Diego LBC | $225 (adults), $100 (youth) |
| Lindsay LBC (Canada) | $185 (regular), $150 (new) |
| Santa Barbara LBC | $155 |
| Redlands LBC | $120 |
| Berkeley LBC | Listed on site, typically $150-200 range |
| Jefferson Park (Seattle) | Listed on site |

**Typical range: $120-$235/year**, which includes Bowls USA dues (~$25), division dues, and club operating costs.

**Source confidence:** HIGH -- direct from club websites

### DEI Pricing Recommendation

Given that bowlers already pay $120-$235/year for membership, and the sport event insurance market shows per-participant costs of $2-$10:

**Per-Event (Primary Product):**

| Tier | Price | Coverage | Target |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Basic** | $3-$5 per session | Activity injury medical + emergency transport | Casual visitors, social bowlers, open-day participants |
| **Standard** | $7-$10 per session | Basic + per-participant liability + AD&D | Regular club members, pennant players |
| **Premium** | $12-$15 per session | Standard + enhanced medical limits + equipment | Tournament players, traveling competitors |

**Rationale:**
- At $5 per session, a bowler who plays 2x/week for 6 months spends $260/year -- comparable to club membership fees. This is the upper boundary.
- At $3 per session, that same bowler spends $156/year -- right in the membership fee range but providing actual medical coverage.
- The sweet spot is $5-$7 per session for the standard tier. Most bowlers will rationalize this as "less than a coffee."
- For elderly users on fixed incomes, $3-$5 must be the visible anchor price.

**Tournament Day Insurance (High-Value Product):**

| Option | Price | Coverage | Target |
|--------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Individual tournament day** | $8-$12 per player | Full standard coverage for tournament duration | Individual competitors |
| **Club tournament package** | $5-$8 per player (bulk) | Full coverage for all registered players | Club purchasing for the whole field |
| **Open day / come-and-try** | $3-$5 per visitor | Basic coverage for newcomers and visitors | Clubs hosting recruitment events |

**Rationale:**
- Tournament entry fees typically run $10-$30 per player. Adding $8-$12 for insurance is a 30-50% increase, but covers the highest-risk events (unfamiliar greens, competitive intensity, travel fatigue).
- Club-purchased bulk coverage at $5-$8/player is the most likely conversion path -- club secretaries buy it for everyone.

**Annual Coverage Option (Secondary Product):**

| Tier | Price | Coverage |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Individual annual** | $75-$120/year | All sessions at home club + 10 away events |
| **Club annual** | $40-$60/member/year (bulk) | All members, all sessions, all events |

**Rationale:**
- Annual pricing must undercut the per-event math to incentivize commitment
- At $75/year vs $5/session x 50 sessions = $250/year, the annual is a clear win for regulars
- Club bulk at $40-$60/member makes it trivially easy for clubs to add to membership fees

### Revenue Model for the User (DEI Owner)

Since the user owns DEI, the revenue pipeline is:

1. **Pick-a-Partner app triggers insurance offer** at check-in or tournament registration
2. **Player pays per-event or annual premium** directly to DEI
3. **DEI retains premium minus carrier/underwriting costs** (typically 30-40% retained as MGA margin)
4. **Venue/club earns referral fee** ($2-$10 per participant, as already stated on the insurance page)
5. **Pick-a-Partner platform earns nothing directly** -- it IS the distribution channel for DEI

**The business model is vertical integration:**
- Pick-a-Partner = distribution channel (free to venues, drives adoption)
- DEI = insurance product (monetization layer)
- The app exists to create the check-in moment where insurance is sold
- Every new club on Pick-a-Partner = new insurance distribution point

**Estimated revenue per club:**
- Average club: 50 active members, playing 1.5x/week average
- At 30% conversion to $5/session insurance: 50 * 1.5 * 0.3 * $5 = $112.50/week per club
- Annually per club: ~$5,850 gross premium
- At 35% MGA margin: ~$2,047 net revenue per club per year
- At 100 clubs: ~$204,700/year

## 4. Microsite Design for Lawn Bowls DEI

### Critical UX Consideration: The Demographic

This is NOT a typical tech-savvy user base.
- 92% of lawn bowlers are over 45
- The core demographic is 65-80 year olds
- Many are on fixed incomes
- Vision impairment affects 1 in 6 people over 70
- Cognitive load tolerance is lower
- Trust and legitimacy signals matter MORE for this demographic

**Source confidence:** HIGH -- Nielsen Norman Group research on senior UX design, Victorian health data

### Landing Page Structure

**Section 1: Hero**
- Headline: "Play Protected. Every Bowl, Every Session."
- Subtext: Specific to lawn bowls, not generic sports. Mention falls, sprains, and bowl-strike injuries by name.
- Primary CTA: "Get Covered in 60 Seconds" (large button, high contrast)
- Trust badges immediately visible: AIG, Lloyd's, Great American logos
- Price anchor: "From $3 per session"

**Section 2: "Why Bowlers Need This"**
- Stats from Monash research (falls = 54% of injuries, fractures = 35%)
- "Your club's insurance covers the CLUB. It doesn't cover YOU."
- Scenario: "Margaret trips on the ditch bank, fractures her wrist. Without per-player coverage, that's her $12,000 problem."
- Use bowling-specific language: greens, jacks, ditches, ends, rinks

**Section 3: "What's Covered"**
- Same 4 categories as existing page but with lawn-bowls-specific examples:
  - Per-Participant Liability: "Bowl strikes another player's foot? Covered."
  - Activity Injury Medical: "Strained your back on the delivery? Covered."
  - Emergency Transport: "Need an ambulance from the green? Covered."
  - ActiveGuard AD&D: "The coverage nobody wants to need. But it's there."

**Section 4: "How It Works"**
- 3 steps, LARGE text, ONE action per step
- Step 1: "Check in at your club through Pick a Partner"
- Step 2: "Tap 'Get Covered' when it pops up"
- Step 3: "Play with peace of mind. You're covered."
- Emphasize: NO forms, NO phone calls, NO waiting

**Section 5: Coverage Tiers**
- Simple 3-column comparison (Basic / Standard / Premium)
- Clear pricing per session AND annual option
- "Most Popular" badge on Standard
- "Best Value" badge on Annual

**Section 6: "For Club Secretaries"**
- Club-wide coverage option
- "Cover your whole club for the season" pitch
- Dashboard preview: see which members are covered
- Bulk pricing calculator
- "Add to membership fees" suggestion

**Section 7: FAQ**
- Tailored to lawn bowls concerns:
  - "I'm 78. Can I get coverage?" (Yes, no age limit for per-event)
  - "Does this cover me at away matches?" (Yes, any participating venue)
  - "My club already has insurance. Why do I need this?" (Club insurance covers the club, not you)
  - "What if I just play social bowls, not pennant?" (Covered regardless of competition level)
  - "Can my club buy this for all members?" (Yes, club packages available)

**Section 8: CTA + Trust**
- Final large CTA
- Carrier logos
- "Licensed in all 50 states"
- Testimonial or endorsement (seek from Bowls USA or division contacts)

### Quote Flow UX for Elderly Users

Based on Nielsen Norman Group and Smashing Magazine research on senior-friendly design:

**Principles:**
1. **One question per screen.** Never present multiple form fields simultaneously
2. **Minimum 18px body text, 24px+ for form labels.** Larger is always better
3. **High contrast ratios** (WCAG AAA, not just AA). Black text on white, not gray-on-gray
4. **No icons without labels.** Every icon gets a text description
5. **No infinite scrolling.** Pagination with clear "Page 2 of 4" indicators
6. **Large tap targets.** Minimum 48px, prefer 56px+ for primary actions
7. **No auto-advancing.** User controls when to move to the next step
8. **Clear error messages** that explain what went wrong AND how to fix it
9. **Progress indicator** showing "Step 2 of 4" at all times
10. **Phone number prominently displayed** for those who want human help

**Recommended Quote Flow (4 screens max):**

Screen 1: "Who are you?"
- First name, Last name (pre-filled if logged into Pick-a-Partner)
- Date of birth (dropdown selectors, NOT a date picker widget)
- "Next" button (large, high contrast)

Screen 2: "Where do you play?"
- Club name (dropdown of participating clubs, or type-to-search)
- State (dropdown)
- "Next" button

Screen 3: "Choose your coverage"
- 3 tier cards (Basic / Standard / Premium) with prices
- Clear bullet points of what each includes
- "Select" button per tier
- "Not sure? Call us at (XXX) XXX-XXXX"

Screen 4: "Confirm and pay"
- Summary of what they selected
- Total price clearly shown
- Payment (Apple Pay / Google Pay for one-tap, plus credit card)
- "Get Covered" button
- Confirmation screen with PDF certificate download

### Co-Branding with Pick-a-Partner

The microsite should make the relationship clear:
- Pick-a-Partner logo in nav alongside DEI branding
- "Powered by Daily Event Insurance" badge
- URL structure: `dailyeventinsurance.com/m/pick-a-partner/lawn-bowls/`
- Or a dedicated subdomain: `bowls.dailyeventinsurance.com`
- The lawn-bowls microsite should FEEL different from the general pickleball-focused page -- green/earth tones instead of teal, imagery of lawn bowling not pickleball

## 5. Integration with Pick-a-Partner

### Current State

The app already has:
- Insurance page at `/insurance` (reviewed -- currently pickleball-focused)
- Links to `dailyeventinsurance.com/m/pick-a-partner/quote/new`
- Waiver flow that precedes insurance offer
- Check-in system (QR code venue check-in, per recent commit)
- Tournament bracket system (round-robin, per recent commit)

### Integration Points

**Point 1: Tournament Registration**
When a player registers for a lawn bowls tournament through Pick-a-Partner:
- After registration confirmation, show insurance offer
- "You're registered for the Saturday Pairs. Want to play protected?"
- Pre-fill all quote info from their profile (name, DOB, club)
- One-tap purchase with saved payment method

**Point 2: Check-In (Highest Conversion Point)**
When a player checks in via QR code at the venue:
- If NOT covered for today: "You're not covered today. Get covered for $5?"
- If covered: green shield badge, "You're covered today"
- This is the moment of highest urgency -- they're about to play
- Conversion should be highest here

**Point 3: Club Admin Dashboard**
For club secretaries/administrators:
- Dashboard showing coverage status of all members
- Color-coded: green (covered today), yellow (coverage expired), red (never covered)
- Bulk purchase option: "Cover all 32 players for Saturday's tournament -- $160"
- Monthly/annual coverage reports
- Revenue tracking (if club earns referral fees)

**Point 4: Season/Annual Enrollment**
During annual membership renewal:
- "Add DEI coverage to your membership for $60/year"
- Club can include it as a line item in membership fees
- Automatic coverage for all events at home club + away matches

### Revenue Pipeline (DEI Owner Perspective)

```
Player checks in at club
    --> App shows insurance offer
        --> Player purchases ($5)
            --> DEI processes payment
                --> Carrier gets ~60-65% (underwriting + claims reserve)
                --> DEI retains ~35-40% gross margin
                    --> Club referral fee: $2-$3 (from DEI margin)
                    --> DEI net: $0.50-$1.50 per transaction
                    --> Pick-a-Partner: $0 direct (it IS the DEI channel)
```

At scale, the math works because:
- Volume is the game, not margin per transaction
- 100 clubs x 50 members x 1.5 sessions/week x 30% conversion x $5 = $1.125M gross premium/year
- At 35% margin: ~$393K gross revenue for DEI
- Minus club referral fees (~$225K): ~$168K net
- This scales linearly with club count

### Club-Wide vs Per-Player Coverage

| Model | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-------|------|------|----------|
| **Per-player per-session** | No commitment, pay-as-you-play, visitors included | Lower total revenue per club, conversion friction each session | Social bowlers, visitors, casual players |
| **Club-wide annual** | Predictable revenue, 100% coverage, easy for admin | Higher upfront cost for club, clubs may resist adding to fees | Established clubs with strong management |
| **Individual annual** | Best value for regular players, set-and-forget | Player must opt in individually, lower casual participation coverage | Dedicated pennant/competition players |

**Recommendation:** Lead with per-session at check-in (highest urgency, lowest friction), then upsell annual after 5-10 purchases ("You've spent $35 on session coverage this month. Annual coverage is $75 for the whole year -- save 70%").

## 6. Competitive Positioning

### Why DEI Wins in Lawn Bowls

| Factor | Traditional Insurance | DEI |
|--------|----------------------|-----|
| **Purchase friction** | Call broker, get quote, fill forms, wait days | One tap at check-in, covered in seconds |
| **Coverage scope** | Club-level liability only | Per-participant: liability + medical + transport + AD&D |
| **Visitor coverage** | None (non-members excluded) | Anyone who checks in can purchase |
| **Tournament coverage** | Separate event policy ($100-$400) | Per-player, included with check-in |
| **Cost transparency** | Opaque annual premium, club pays | Clear per-session price, player or club pays |
| **Age friendliness** | Paper forms, phone calls | Digital but designed for seniors (large text, simple flow) |
| **Integration** | Standalone policy, no connection to play | Embedded in the check-in/play workflow |

### Potential Objections and Responses

**"Our club already has insurance."**
Response: Your club's GL covers the club entity. It does NOT cover individual player injuries during play. If Margaret fractures her hip on a slippery green, your club's policy protects the club from Margaret's lawsuit -- it doesn't pay Margaret's medical bills. DEI covers Margaret.

**"Our members can't afford another fee."**
Response: $3-$5 per session is less than the post-game drinks at the clubhouse bar. And it's optional -- no one is forced to buy. Club-wide annual at $40-$60/member can be absorbed into existing membership fees.

**"Bowlers don't get seriously hurt. It's a gentle sport."**
Response: Monash University data shows 35% of lawn bowls injuries are FRACTURES. Falls account for 54% of all injuries. The average age of participants is over 65. A fall for a 70-year-old is not the same as a fall for a 30-year-old.

**"We've never had a claim."**
Response: That's great. Insurance isn't for what has happened -- it's for what could happen. The first time a visitor trips on the green bank and breaks a wrist, the club is exposed. Per-participant coverage eliminates that exposure for $5.

## 7. Lawn Bowls Tournament Formats (Context for DEI Product Design)

Understanding tournament formats matters because each format has different participant counts and risk profiles:

| Format | Players per Team | Bowls per Player | Typical Duration | Insurance Consideration |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|------------------|------------------------|
| **Singles** | 1 | 4 | 2-3 hours | Individual coverage only |
| **Pairs** | 2 | 4 | 2-3 hours | Both players need coverage |
| **Triples** | 3 | 3 | 2-3 hours | 6 players per rink |
| **Fours** | 4 | 2 | 2-3 hours | 8 players per rink, most common competitive format |
| **Australian Pairs** | 2 | Varies (2-4-2 format) | 2-3 hours | Popular variation |
| **Mixed Pairs/Fours** | 2-4 (mixed gender) | Varies | 2-3 hours | Growing format |

**Tournament sizes:**
- Club social tournament: 16-32 players
- Inter-club competition (pennant): 24-48 players per day
- Division/regional championship: 50-200 players
- National championship: 200+ players

**Implication for DEI:** Tournament-day packages should be priced per player, with club secretaries able to purchase for the entire field in one transaction. A "32 players x $8 = $256" checkout is the ideal scenario.
