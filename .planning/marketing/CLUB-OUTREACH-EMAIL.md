# Club Outreach Email Templates

Three email templates for club acquisition. Keep them personal — these are volunteers, not procurement managers.

---

## Email 1: Cold Outreach to Club Secretary

**Subject:** A free app to take the hassle out of bowling day — built for clubs like yours

Hi [First Name],

I'm reaching out because I know how much work goes into running a bowling day — the draw, the check-in sheets, chasing scorecards, posting results. Most of it still happens on paper, and it all falls on a few good volunteers.

We've built a free app called LawnBowl that handles the repetitive parts:

- **Check-in:** Players tap their name on a tablet at the door. No sign-in sheets.
- **The draw:** You generate it in the app and it goes straight to every player's phone. No crowding around the noticeboard.
- **Scoring:** Players score end-by-end on their phones. No more illegible pencil cards.
- **Results:** Automatically tallied and available instantly.

It's designed for bowlers, not tech enthusiasts. We've tested it with members in their 80s and they picked it up in minutes.

I'd love to give you a quick 15-minute walkthrough over the phone. No obligation — just a demo so you can see if it'd suit your club.

Would any day this week work for a quick chat?

Cheers,
[Your Name]
LawnBowl — www.lawnbowling.app

1. **Create your club** at www.lawnbowling.app/register (takes about 5 minutes)
2. **Add your members** — you can import from a spreadsheet or add them one by one
3. **Run a trial day** — I'd suggest a quiet social day first, not pennant Saturday

I'm happy to be on the phone during your first bowling day in case anything comes up. Just pick a date and let me know.

A few clubs have told us the draw alone saves them 15 minutes every session. Multiply that by three days a week and you're getting a full hour back.

Any questions at all, just reply to this email.
