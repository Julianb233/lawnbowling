> We built an app for lawn bowlers — by lawn bowlers.
>
> After years of paper draw sheets, illegible scorecards, and players crowding around the noticeboard to find their rink, we decided there had to be a better way.
>

>
> - Draw the rinks and send assignments straight to players' phones
> - Score end-by-end on your phone — just like the pencil card, but everyone can see it

> - Live scores on the clubhouse TV

## 4. LinkedIn - Professional

> Lawn bowls is one of the most popular participation sports in Australia and the UK, with thousands of clubs run almost entirely by volunteers.
>
> Yet most clubs still manage their operations with clipboards, paper draw sheets, and noticeboards. The result: volunteer burnout, lost records, and a participation experience that hasn't changed in decades.
>
> Today we're launching LawnBowl — a purpose-built platform that digitises the core operations of a lawn bowling club: player check-in, draw generation, live scoring, and results management.
>
> What makes it different from generic sports apps:
>
> - It mirrors the language and workflow bowlers already know (ends, rinks, skips — not "innings" or "courts")
> - It's designed for an older demographic with large tap targets, high contrast, and zero clutter
> - Clubs can run a kiosk on a tablet at the door, cast scores to a TV, and let players follow along on their phones
> - No training required for players — the draw just appears on their screen
>
> We're partnering with clubs across Australia for a free rollout.
>
> If you work in community sport, club governance, or sports technology, I'd welcome a conversation.
>
