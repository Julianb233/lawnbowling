Lawn Bowling Configurations Reference (All Formats)

Fours (Rinks) — 4v4

2 bowls per player. 4 positions: Lead, Second, Vice/Third, Skip. Standard competitive format used in Pennant leagues.

8 players/rink. 500 members = 62 rinks needed. Draw: Mead or Gavel rotation tables.

Triples — 3v3

3 bowls per player. 3 positions: Lead, Second, Skip. Most popular social format worldwide.

6 players/rink. 500 members = 83 rinks. Draw: Random or Seeded.

Pairs — 2v2

4 bowls per player. 2 positions: Lead, Skip. Faster games, great for beginners.

4 players/rink. 500 members = 125 rinks. Draw: Random.

Singles — 1v1

4 bowls per player. No positions. Practice or competitive ranking format.

2 players/rink. 500 members = 250 rinks. Usually limited entry.

Mead Draw System

Pre-computed rotation tables ensuring every player faces different opponents across rounds. Best for multi-round social bowls.

Supports specific player counts (16, 20, 24, etc.). Falls back to random for unsupported counts.

Gavel Draw System

Fours-only rotation tables. Used in formal competition and championship qualifying events.

Limited to Fours format. Pre-defined rotation patterns.

Seeded Draw

Position-based assignment using skill ratings. Balances rinks by distributing experienced Skips evenly.

Uses ELO ratings per position. Skill Balance weighs 40% in team-assignment-engine.

Pennant League (Round Robin)

Competitive season play: Divisions, fixtures, home/away. Circle method scheduling. Points: W=2, D=1, L=0.

Standings: Points > Shot Diff > Shots For > Rink Wins. Season states: draft/reg/in-progress/complete.
