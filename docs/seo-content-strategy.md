# lawnbowl.app — SEO Content Strategy

**Prepared:** March 11, 2026
**Linear:** AI-2406
**Objective:** Dominate lawn bowling search results globally and convert organic traffic into platform signups.

---

## Executive Summary

Lawn bowling is a niche sport with a fragmented online content ecosystem. Most existing content is produced by governing bodies (Bowls USA, Bowls England, World Bowls), club websites with thin content, and a handful of independent enthusiast sites. Very few sites are consistently publishing high-quality, modern content. This is a significant opportunity.

lawnbowl.app occupies a unique position: a modern, global platform that can become the authoritative online resource for lawn bowling knowledge — driving organic discovery, building trust, and converting readers into paying club administrators and active members.

The content strategy operates on three levels:

1. **Top of funnel** — Educate and attract beginners, curious outsiders, and people searching for local clubs
2. **Middle of funnel** — Engage existing players with technique, tactics, and format guides
3. **Bottom of funnel** — Convert club administrators searching for tournament management software

## 1. Content Gap Analysis

### What exists online

| Content Type | Existing Coverage | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| Basic rules explanations | Multiple sites (Bowls USA, Jack High Bowls, RulesOfSport) | Moderate — mostly text walls, poor UX |
| History of lawn bowling | Several long-form articles | Reasonable but dated |
| Equipment buying guides | Jack High Bowls has strong coverage | Good — opportunity to compete |
| Club finder / directory | Bowls USA, LawnBowls.com, regional associations | Fragmented, often outdated |
| Tournament format explanations | Almost nothing accessible | Major gap |
| Club management / operations | Nearly zero public content | Huge gap |
| Draw tournament "how to run" | A few PDF guides on club sites | Major gap |
| Barefoot bowls / corporate events | Australian venues only | Gap for global audience |
| Player position deep dives | Some club PDFs, poor discoverability | Gap |
| Green maintenance | UK lawn care sites, not bowls-specific | Gap for club operators |
| Scoring system comparisons | Almost nothing | Major gap |
| Software / technology in bowls | Zero editorial content | Unique opportunity |

### Critical content gaps to own

1. **Tournament management "how-to" content** — No one publishes modern, practical guides on running draw tournaments, managing rinks, or calculating standings. This directly maps to lawnbowl.app's core product.
2. **Club operations playbook** — Volunteer committee members Google questions like "how to run an AGM" and "how to manage bowling club membership" with no good answers.
3. **Format explainers** — Powerplay, Tiger Skins, Progressive Triples, and other specialty formats are barely documented anywhere online.
4. **"Is lawn bowling for me?"** content — No site serves the curious first-timer well. Most sites assume you already play.
5. **Barefoot bowls business guide** — Clubs want to run corporate events but have no resource for pricing, setup, or marketing.

## 6. Local SEO Strategy

### The opportunity

"Lawn bowling near me" and "[city] lawn bowling club" searches are dominated by Google Maps / Local Pack results. The strategy is two-pronged: (1) rank the lawnbowl.app club directory pages for city-specific terms, and (2) help clubs on the platform rank their own Google Business Profiles.

### Tactics

**Club directory pages**
- Create a `/clubs/[country]/[state]/[city]` URL structure
- Each city page: list of clubs, short description, embedded map, content about the local lawn bowling scene
- Example: `/clubs/us/california/san-diego` targets "San Diego lawn bowling club"
- Optimized H1: "Lawn Bowling Clubs in San Diego, California"
- Include: club name, address, hours, green count, website, whether they accept visitors/beginners

**State / region hub pages**
- `/clubs/us/california` — "Lawn Bowling Clubs in California" — aggregates all CA city pages
- `/clubs/australia/new-south-wales` — etc.
- These rank for broad state-level searches and pass link equity to city pages

**Schema markup**
- Use `SportsClub` and `SportsOrganization` schema on all club directory pages
- Use `LocalBusiness` schema on club profile pages generated from platform data
- FAQ schema on all educational content

**Google Business Profile (for lawnbowl.app itself)**
- Category: "Software Company" + "Sports Management Software"
- Posts: Repurpose blog content as GBP posts weekly
- Reviews: Solicit reviews from club administrators after onboarding

**Help clubs optimize their GBPs**
- Publish a guide: "How to Set Up Google My Business for Your Lawn Bowling Club"
- Offer a free "Club SEO Checklist" as a lead magnet
- This content targets the club administrator audience and builds goodwill

### Target city pages (Priority 1 — High population + known bowling culture)

**United States:** San Diego, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Seattle, Portland, Denver, Phoenix, Chicago, New York, Boston, Miami, Tampa, St. Petersburg

**Australia:** Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide (strong bowls culture)


### Schema markup priorities

- `Article` schema on all blog posts
- `FAQPage` schema on rules, etiquette, beginner pages
- `HowTo` schema on technique guides
- `SportsClub` + `LocalBusiness` schema on club directory pages
- `SoftwareApplication` schema on product pages
- `BreadcrumbList` on all pages

### Content hub architecture (internal linking)

Build topic clusters:
- **Beginner Hub** → links to rules, etiquette, positions, equipment guide, find-a-club
- **Tournament Hub** → links to all format guides, drawmaster guide, software comparison
- **Club Operations Hub** → links to revenue guide, barefoot bowls, equipment, management software
- **Technique Hub** → links to all position guides, delivery guide, tactics

Each hub page is a pillar piece (2,500-4,000 words) that links to all supporting cluster pages. Supporting pages link back to the hub and to each other contextually.

## 9. Content Production Guidelines

### Voice and tone

- **Authoritative but accessible** — Write like a knowledgeable friend, not a government website
- **Optimistic and modern** — Counter the "old person's game" stereotype; the sport is vibrant and growing
- **Practical first** — Lead with actionable information, not history or context
- **Inclusive** — Address beginners, experienced players, and club administrators in different sections
- **Avoid jargon without explanation** — Define terms on first use, always link to the glossary

### Content standards

- Minimum 1,200 words for any educational post; 2,000+ for pillar content
- Every post needs: target keyword in H1, first 100 words, at least one subheading, and image alt text
- Include a Table of Contents on posts over 1,500 words
- End every post with a clear CTA relevant to the post topic
- Update evergreen content annually with a "Last Updated" date
- Use original images, diagrams, and illustrations where possible — lawn bowling has almost no high-quality visual content online
