**Last Updated:** March 11, 2026
**Sources:** Bowls Australia National Census, Bowls England Annual Reports, Bowls New Zealand Annual Reports, World Bowls, Bowls Canada Boulingrin, Bowls South Africa, Pew Research Center, AARP Tech Trends, Sport England, and various national governing body publications.

## 1. Global Overview

### Total Participation Worldwide

Lawn bowls is played in over 50 countries across six continents, with World Bowls recognizing 54 Member National Authorities in 51 countries. While no single authoritative global figure exists, aggregating national data yields an estimated **2-3 million active participants worldwide**, with casual and social players pushing the broader participation figure significantly higher.

**Key participation figures by country (registered + casual):**

| Country | Registered Members | Casual/Social Players | Total Participation | Clubs |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|--------------------|----|
| Australia | ~138,000 | ~500,000+ | ~644,000 (incl. promotional) | 1,774 |
| United Kingdom (England) | ~100,000 | ~170,000 | ~269,000 | 2,700 |
| New Zealand | ~30,000 (est.) | ~124,000 | ~154,000 | 460 |
| South Africa | ~23,000 | Unknown | ~30,000+ | 513 |
| Canada | ~13,000 | Unknown | ~20,000+ | 200 |
| United States | ~2,800 | Unknown | ~5,000+ | ~75 |

*Sources: Bowls Australia National Census 2022-23, Bowls England, World Bowls, Bowls NZ Facilities Strategy 2024, Bowls Canada Boulingrin, Bowls South Africa, Bowls USA*

### Growth & Decline Trends

The story of lawn bowls is one of **declining formal membership but growing casual participation:**

- **Australia:** Registered membership has declined at -3.98% annually since 2010, but total participation (including social bowls) has grown at +1.75% annually. Competition bowls saw a +13.5% per annum increase recently -- the first in years. Promotional bowls experiences exceeded 1.28 million in 2022-23, with 76% from barefoot bowling.
- **United Kingdom:** Approximately 269,000 participants in England (2023), with numbers increasing slightly in 2024. Player numbers in disability bowls grew 30% in 2025. Competition entries more than doubled. The sport received a significant boost from inclusion in the Glasgow 2026 Commonwealth Games.
- **New Zealand:** Casual playing numbers reached 123,876 in 2024, exceeding pre-COVID figures. Youth participation grew from 3,828 in 2010 to 9,901 in 2024. However, formal club membership continues to contract -- from 44,923 members across 583 clubs in 2010 to an estimated ~30,000 across 460 clubs today.
- **General trend:** The sport is transitioning from a membership-based model to a participation-based model. Social and barefoot bowls are driving growth while traditional pennant membership declines. This has major implications for digital products.

### Countries That Dominate the Sport

**Tier 1 -- Major lawn bowls nations:**
1. **Australia** -- Largest participation base, strongest commercial infrastructure, most developed social bowls sector
2. **United Kingdom** (England, Scotland, Wales) -- Deep heritage, large club network, strong competition scene
3. **New Zealand** -- Highest per-capita participation, strong community integration

**Tier 2 -- Significant presence:**
4. **South Africa** -- 23,000+ registered players, strong competition tradition
5. **Canada** -- Growing slowly, bilingual governance (Bowls Canada Boulingrin)
6. **Scotland, Wales, Ireland** -- Independent from Bowls England, separate governing bodies

**Tier 3 -- Emerging/niche:**
7. **Hong Kong** -- Active competitive scene, hosts World Cup events
8. **Malaysia** -- Dominant in Asian championships, growing junior program
9. **Singapore, Japan, India** -- Small but organized
10. **United States** -- Very small, concentrated in specific regions (Sun Belt, Pacific Northwest)

- Clubs exist across metropolitan, regional, and rural areas

- Rural/regional clubs are the social hub of their communities but face membership pressure

**Socioeconomic Status:**
- Lawn bowls historically draws from middle to lower-middle income demographics
- Club membership fees are relatively affordable ($150-$400/year)

1. Social connection and community
2. Physical activity that is low-impact and accessible

4. Getting outdoors

2. Barefoot bowling social events

- Resistance to new apps exists but is decreasing year-over-year

**Peak Playing Seasons:**
- **Main season:** September/October to April/May (Southern Hemisphere summer)
- **Peak competition:** October to March
- **Social bowls:** Year-round in warmer states, seasonal in southern states
- **Indoor bowls:** Available year-round in some states

**Urban vs Rural:**
- Strong in suburban areas and market towns
- Village greens historically central to the sport
- Urban clubs face pressure from property values and redevelopment

- Club fees typically GBP 80-200/year

**Key Motivations:**

2. Social belonging and friendship
3. Outdoor activity during English summer

**How They Discover the Sport:**
1. Family tradition
2. Bowls' Big Weekend (annual national recruitment event -- record participation in 2024)
3. Walking past a local green
4. Retirement and seeking activities
5. Community center or parish events

**Technology Adoption:**
- Similar to Australia: high smartphone ownership in 60-69 bracket
- Facebook dominant for club communication
- WhatsApp groups increasingly used within clubs
- Bowls England uses digital platforms for results and competition entry
- Some resistance to digital-only communication among oldest members

**Peak Playing Seasons:**
- **Outdoor season:** April to September (weather-dependent, sometimes extended)
- **Indoor season:** October to March
- **Key events:** Bowls' Big Weekend (May/June), National Finals (August/September)

## 3. The User Avatar

### Persona 1: The Retiree (60-75)

**The most common lawn bowler worldwide.**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age Range** | 60-75 |
| **Gender** | 65% male, but female segment growing |
| **Life Stage** | Recently retired or semi-retired |
| **Income** | Fixed income (pension/superannuation), moderate means |
| **Location** | Suburban or regional town, near a bowling club |
| **Marital Status** | Often married; sometimes recently widowed |
| **Health** | Generally good for age; may have minor mobility issues |

**Technology Comfort:**
- Owns a smartphone (81% of 60-69 year-olds do)
- Uses it primarily for calls, texts, photos, Facebook, and weather
- Comfortable with WhatsApp and basic apps
- Can download and use apps but may need initial guidance
- Prefers larger text and simple navigation
- Trust is critical -- will not use apps that feel "dodgy"

**Motivations for Playing:**
1. Social connection after losing workplace relationships
2. Structured routine and purpose in retirement
3. Physical activity that does not strain joints
4. Competition and mental stimulation
5. Being outdoors

**Pain Points:**
1. Hard to know when and where games are happening at their club
2. Keeping track of scores and results manually
3. Difficulty finding partners/fill-ins for team games
4. Club communication is fragmented (noticeboard, phone tree, Facebook)
5. Feeling excluded from decisions or information

**What They Would Value in an App:**
- Easy-to-read schedule of games and events at their club
- Simple score tracking during games
- Results history and ladder/standings
- Club news and announcements in one place
- Member directory with contact info
- Push notifications for cancellations (weather) or schedule changes

**Barriers to App Adoption:**
- "Why do I need another app?"
- Fear of doing something wrong or breaking something
- Privacy concerns about personal information
- Small text and confusing navigation
- No one showing them how to use it
- Lack of perceived value until they see others using it

**How to Reach Them:**
- Through their bowling club (club champion/secretary endorsement)
- Facebook community groups
- In-person demonstration at club events
- Word of mouth from other bowlers
- Local newspaper or club newsletter

### Persona 2: The Active Senior (75+)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age Range** | 75-90 |
| **Gender** | 60% male, 40% female |
| **Life Stage** | Deep into retirement, bowls is a life anchor |
| **Income** | Fixed pension, modest |
| **Health** | May have vision, hearing, or mobility limitations |

**Technology Comfort:**
- 62% own a smartphone, but many underutilize it
- Some still use feature phones
- Relies on family members for tech support
- Very low tolerance for complexity
- May use a tablet at home but not a phone for apps
- Facebook usage is moderate; YouTube is popular for entertainment

**Motivations for Playing:**
1. Bowls is their primary social life
2. Maintaining independence and mobility
3. Long-standing friendships at the club
4. Routine and structure
5. Competition -- many elite seniors are in their 70s-80s

**Pain Points:**
1. Difficulty reading small screens
2. Club communication moves online but they miss out
3. Feeling left behind as technology changes
4. Transport to and from the club
5. Health issues limiting play days

**What They Would Value in an App:**
- Very large text with high contrast
- Extremely simple: "What games are on this week?"
- Voice-based features (text-to-speech for announcements)
- A family member or club volunteer could set it up for them
- Push notifications with just essential information

**Barriers to App Adoption:**
- Low tech confidence; fear of "breaking" things
- Small screen size; vision impairments
- Complexity overwhelm -- any friction means abandonment
- May not have data plans or Wi-Fi understanding
- "I've managed without it for years"

**How to Reach Them:**
- Through club volunteers and committee members
- Family members (adult children/grandchildren)
- In-person demonstrations with patient, one-on-one help
- Print materials (flyers, club newsletters) directing to the app
- Must NOT be digital-only outreach

### UX Considerations for the 60+ Demographic

**Visual Design:**
- Minimum 16px font size; ideally 18-20px for body text
- Allow user-adjustable text sizing
- High contrast ratios (minimum WCAG AA, ideally AAA: 7:1)
- Avoid blue/purple and yellow/green color pairs (hard to distinguish with aging eyes)
- Clear, bold icons with descriptive text labels (never icons alone)
- Central placement of key interactive elements

**Navigation:**
- Maximum 2-3 levels of navigation depth
- Persistent "Home" button always visible
- Minimize sub-menus
- Large tap targets (minimum 44x44px, ideally 48x48px)
- Clear "Back" navigation
- Avoid swipe gestures as primary navigation

**Interaction:**
- Simple, linear flows (one task at a time)
- Confirmation screens before important actions
- Error messages in plain language with clear recovery steps
- Progress indicators for multi-step processes
- Generous timeout periods
- Avoid auto-scrolling or content that moves unexpectedly

**Emotional/Trust:**
- 60% of older users avoid new apps due to fear of "doing something wrong"
- Visible security indicators build trust
- Confirmation dialogs prevent anxiety about accidental actions
- Familiar patterns (similar to apps they already use)
- Patient, encouraging error handling

**Accessibility:**
- Voice-based controls and audio feedback for vision/motor impairments
- Support for system-level accessibility settings (dynamic type, VoiceOver)
- Compatibility with hearing aids
- Option for high-contrast mode

### Barriers to App Adoption for Seniors

| Barrier | % Citing as Issue | Mitigation |
|---------|------------------|------------|
| Privacy/data concerns | 33% | Clear privacy policy, minimal data collection |
| Ease of use | 20% | Simple UX, in-person onboarding support |
| Setup and support | 19% | Club volunteer "digital champions" |
| Cost | 16% | Free tier essential |
| Awareness | 12% | Multi-channel promotion (not digital-only) |
| Fear of doing something wrong | 60% (broader) | Forgiving UI, undo options, confirmations |

*Source: AARP 2024 Tech Trends survey*

### Messaging That Resonates

**For Retirees and Seniors:**
- "Stay connected to your club" (not "download our app")
- "Never miss a game" (practical value proposition)
- "Your club in your pocket" (familiar concept)
- "Made for bowlers, by bowlers" (trust and authenticity)
- Avoid: tech jargon, "revolutionary," "disruptive"

**For Newcomers:**
- "Find a game near you"
- "Bowls is for everyone"
- "The sport you didn't know you'd love"
- "Join 500,000+ Australians on the green" (social proof)
- Avoid: making it seem too serious or traditional

**For Competitive Players:**
- "Track your stats. Improve your game."
- "Real-time scores and results"
- "Your competition dashboard"
- Lead with data and performance features

**For Social Players:**
- "Organize your bowls group in seconds"
- "Who's in for Saturday?"
- "Your bowls social life, simplified"
- Lead with group coordination and convenience

### Key Marketing Principles

1. **Club-first strategy:** Individual bowlers will adopt what their club adopts. Win the club secretary and committee first.
2. **Bottom-up, not top-down:** Grassroots adoption within friend groups and social circles drives sustained usage.
3. **Print + Digital:** Never rely on digital-only outreach for the 70+ demographic. Flyers, posters in club notice boards, and newsletter inserts are essential.
4. **Show, don't tell:** In-person demonstrations at clubs during social bowls afternoons will convert more users than any digital ad.
5. **Champion program:** Identify "digital champions" at each club -- tech-comfortable members who help others.
6. **Free is essential:** The core product must be free. This demographic will not pay upfront for unproven apps. Monetize through premium features, club subscriptions, or governing body partnerships.
7. **Patience:** Adoption will be slower than typical app launches. Plan for 12-18 month adoption curves, not weeks.
