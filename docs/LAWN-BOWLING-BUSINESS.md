# Lawn Bowling — Business Model & Club Operations

## Club Revenue Streams

| Revenue Source | Typical Range |
|----------------|--------------|
| Membership fees | $120-$400/yr depending on country |
| Green fees (visitors) | ~$25/guest |
| Tournament entry | $3-$25/person |
| Bar & food | Major revenue line (AU/UK) |
| Corporate/barefoot bowls | Growing fast, especially Australia |
| Sponsorship | Local business signage deals |

## Club Governance (Volunteer-Run)

- **President** — Chair of Committee
- **Vice-President**
- **Secretary** — Admin, correspondence
- **Treasurer** — Finance
- **Greens Committee** — Playing surface maintenance
- **Match/Bowls Committee** — Fixtures, draws, team selection
- **Social Committee** — Events, functions

**Key insight:** Users are retirees volunteering their time, not tech professionals. Software must be dead simple.

## Tournament Economics

- Casual tournaments: $3-$10/person entry
- Full-day club tournaments: $10-$25/person (includes meal)
- Prize formula: Teams × Entry Fee − Catering = Pool
- 1st place gets ~50% of pot, 1 prize per 10 entries
- Active clubs run 10-30 tournaments per season

## Demographics

- Dominated by retirees aged 60-80+
- "Young" in bowls means under 40
- Sport has image problem as "old person's game"
- Barefoot bowls attracting younger demographics

## Current Pain Points (Manual Processes)

1. Paper sign-in sheets on clubhouse wall
2. Physical name tags dropped on a table for draws
3. Handwritten scorecards
4. Spreadsheets for tournament management
5. Phone trees for communication
6. Single points of failure (one volunteer holds all knowledge)

## Software Pricing That Works

| Tier | Price | Target |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Free** | $0 | Small clubs, <50 members, basic draw tool |
| **Club** | $30-$50/month | Standard clubs, full management |
| **Premium** | $80-$100/month | Large/multi-green clubs, advanced features |

**Key:** Per-club flat pricing, NOT per-member (penalizes growth).

## The Killer Feature

> The draw tool is the gateway drug. The moment of highest friction in every club is the drawmaster manually creating team draws from whoever showed up. A tool that does this in 10 seconds on a tablet changes everything.
