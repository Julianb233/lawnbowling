## Executive Summary

Lawn bowling (also called "lawn bowls" in Commonwealth countries) occupies a mid-volume, low-competition keyword niche. The term "lawn bowling" dominates in the United States while "lawn bowls" is the standard in Australia, the UK, New Zealand, and South Africa. This dual-term landscape creates an opportunity: most competitor sites optimize for one region's terminology and miss the other.

The sport is experiencing measurable growth in the US and is extremely popular in Australia (240,000+ registered players across 2,000+ clubs) and the UK (3,500+ clubs). Search demand peaks in spring and summer, with a notable secondary spike around event registrations in late winter.

The overall SEO landscape is fragmented — no single dominant media brand owns the space globally. This is a significant content opportunity.

## Search Intent Mapping

### Informational Intent (Learning)

Searchers want to understand the sport, rules, history, or technique. These searches convert to awareness and brand affinity. Priority content types: blog posts, how-to guides, glossaries, FAQ pages, YouTube explainers.

**High-priority informational keywords:**
- `how to play lawn bowls` / `how to play lawn bowling`
- `lawn bowling rules` / `lawn bowls rules`
- `lawn bowling for beginners`
- `what is lawn bowling`
- `lawn bowling history`
- `lawn bowling vs bocce ball`
- `lawn bowls scoring`

- `lawn bowling for seniors`

### Navigational Intent (Finding Clubs and Resources)

Searchers know what they want and are looking for a specific place, club, or official resource. These represent players or potential players actively seeking participation. Priority content types: club directories, location pages, "find a club" tool.

**High-priority navigational keywords:**
- `lawn bowling near me` / `lawn bowls near me`
- `lawn bowling clubs near me`
- `Bowls USA` / `Bowls Australia` / `Bowls England`
- `lawn bowling [city name]`
- `how to join a lawn bowling club`
- `free lawn bowling lessons near me`

### Transactional Intent (Buying Equipment, Joining, Registering)

Searchers are ready to take action — buy equipment, register for a tournament, or join a club. These have the highest commercial value. Priority content types: product pages, comparison guides, affiliate content, club sign-up pages.

**High-priority transactional keywords:**
- `best lawn bowls for beginners`

Searchers are comparing options before committing. These are mid-funnel and bridge informational to transactional. Priority content types: comparison articles, reviews, buyer's guides.

## Seasonal Trends

### When People Search for Lawn Bowling

Based on the sport's nature as an outdoor activity and club patterns, search volume follows a strong seasonal curve.

#### Northern Hemisphere (US, UK, Canada)

| Month | Search Volume | Driver |
|-------|--------------|--------|
| January | Very Low | Off-season, indoor only |
| February | Low | Early planning, membership renewal |
| March | Rising | Season preview, beginner inquiries |
| April | Medium | Season opening, "near me" spikes |
| May | High | Active season, tournaments begin |
| June | Peak | Peak season, highest "near me" searches |
| July | Peak | Peak season, competitions, events |
| August | Peak | Late-season tournaments, nationals |
| September | Medium | Season wind-down, final tournaments |
| October | Low–Medium | Clubs closing, equipment purchases |
| November | Low | Post-season |
| December | Very Low | Off-season |

#### Southern Hemisphere (Australia, New Zealand, South Africa)

Season is approximately inverse — October through March is peak outdoor season. Australia's barefoot bowls scene is heavily active through the summer (December–February) for social events.

### Key Seasonal Moments

- **February–March (US):** "New season" content, membership sign-up articles, "join a lawn bowling club" guides
- **April–May (US/UK):** "Lawn bowling near me," beginner guides, equipment buying guides
- **June–August (US/UK):** Tournament content, competitive keywords, event coverage
- **October (US/AU crossover):** Equipment purchasing season (AU season starting, US season ending); best time for equipment review content
- **December–January (AU):** Barefoot bowls, corporate events, holiday party bowling content

### Content Publishing Strategy by Season

- Publish beginner guides and "what is lawn bowling" content in **January–February** so they index before the spring search surge
- Publish equipment reviews and buying guides in **March–April** (US/UK) and **September–October** (AU)
- Publish tournament and event content **6–8 weeks before** peak event season
- Evergreen content (rules, history, comparisons) can be published year-round but benefits from updates timed to the opening of major seasons

## Geographic Targeting

### United States

- Highest search volume for "lawn bowling" (not "lawn bowls")
- Strong club concentrations in: California, Florida, New York, Colorado, Minnesota, Washington state
- City-level keywords: `lawn bowling Los Angeles`, `lawn bowling San Diego`, `lawn bowling Chicago`, `lawn bowling New York`, `lawn bowling Seattle`, `lawn bowling Denver`
- US clubs heavily represented on Yelp and Google Maps — local SEO critical
- Bowls USA (bowlsusa.us) is the authority but weak on content — large opportunity to rank above them for informational queries

### Australia

- "Lawn bowls" standard (not "lawn bowling")
- Highest raw participation globally (240,000+ registered players, 2,000+ clubs)
- "Barefoot bowls" is a major cultural phenomenon and a large search cluster
- State-level associations: Bowls Victoria, Bowls NSW, Bowls Queensland, etc.
- City keywords: `lawn bowls Sydney`, `lawn bowls Melbourne`, `lawn bowls Brisbane`, `barefoot bowls Melbourne`
- BowlsLink and Bowls Australia have institutional authority but poor consumer content

### United Kingdom

- "Lawn bowls" standard; also "flat green bowls," "crown green bowls," "indoor bowls"
- 3,500+ clubs — large organized base
- Crown green bowls dominates in northern England — distinct sub-niche
- Bowlr.co.uk is the strongest commercial content player in the UK
- City keywords: `lawn bowls London`, `crown green bowls Manchester`, `indoor bowls clubs near me`

### New Zealand, Canada, South Africa

- Smaller but organized markets
- Few strong digital competitors — opportunity for first-mover content targeting
